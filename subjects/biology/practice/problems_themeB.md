**Q18.** **(Fick's Law Calculation — B3.1)**

The rate of diffusion across a gas exchange surface can be modeled by Fick's Law:

$$\text{Rate of diffusion} = k \times \frac{A \times \Delta C}{d}$$

where *k* is the diffusion constant, *A* = surface area, *ΔC* = concentration gradient, and *d* = diffusion distance.

A healthy human alveolus has the following parameters:
- Surface area: 7.0 × 10⁻⁵ m²
- Diffusion distance: 5.0 × 10⁻⁷ m
- Concentration gradient: 8.0 × 10⁻² mol m⁻³
- Diffusion constant *k* = 2.0 × 10⁻⁵ m² s⁻¹

**(a)** Calculate the rate of diffusion for this healthy alveolus. Show your working. [2]

**(b)** A patient with pulmonary fibrosis has a thickened alveolar membrane. The diffusion distance increases to 2.0 × 10⁻⁶ m while all other parameters remain unchanged. Calculate the new rate of diffusion and express it as a percentage of the healthy value. [2]

**(c)** Explain why patients with emphysema experience reduced gas exchange even though their diffusion distance may not increase significantly. [2]
