### Question 1 [1 mark] — Kinematics
A train accelerates uniformly from rest at $2.0~\text{m/s}^2$ for $5.0~\text{s}$, then travels at constant speed for $5.0~\text{s}$, then decelerates uniformly to rest in $5.0~\text{s}$. What is the total distance travelled?

A. $75~\text{m}$  
B. $100~\text{m}$  
C. $125~\text{m}$  
D. $150~\text{m}$

### Question 14 [9 marks] — Kinematics: Projectile Motion
A football is kicked from ground level with an initial speed of $25~\text{m/s}$ at an angle of $37^\circ$ above the horizontal. The ground is level. Neglect air resistance.

(a) Resolve the initial velocity into horizontal and vertical components. **[2 marks]**

(b) Calculate the time of flight (time until the ball returns to ground level). **[2 marks]**

(c) Calculate the horizontal range of the ball. **[2 marks]**

(d) Determine the maximum height reached by the ball. **[2 marks]**

(e) State the speed of the ball just before it hits the ground. Explain your reasoning briefly. **[1 mark]**

### Question 15 [6 marks] — Kinematics: Data Analysis
A student releases a cart from rest on an inclined plane and records its position $x$ at various times $t$:

| $t$ / s | $0$ | $0.50$ | $1.00$ | $1.50$ | $2.00$ | $2.50$ | $3.00$ |
|---------|-----|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| $x$ / m | $0$ | $0.25$ | $1.00$ | $2.25$ | $4.00$ | $6.25$ | $9.00$ |

(a) Show that the displacement is proportional to $t^2$. **[2 marks]**

(b) Hence determine the acceleration of the cart. **[3 marks]**

(c) Given that $a = g\sin\theta$ for a frictionless incline, show that $\sin\theta = 0.20$. **[1 mark]**

### Question 24 [8 marks] — Rigid Body Mechanics: Rolling (HL)
A solid cylinder of mass $4.0~\text{kg}$ and radius $0.20~\text{m}$ rolls **without slipping** down an incline, starting from rest. The cylinder descends a vertical height of $1.2~\text{m}$. The moment of inertia of a solid cylinder about its axis is $I = \frac{1}{2}MR^2$.

(a) By writing the total kinetic energy as the sum of translational and rotational parts, and using the rolling condition $\omega = v/R$, show that the total kinetic energy of the rolling cylinder is $KE = \frac{3}{4}Mv^2$, where $v$ is the translational speed of the centre of mass. **[3 marks]**

(b) Use conservation of energy to calculate the translational speed $v$ of the cylinder at the bottom of the incline. **[3 marks]**

(c) Hence determine the angular velocity $\omega$ of the cylinder at the bottom. **[2 marks]**

### Question 25 [8 marks] — Special Relativity: Muon Dilemma (HL)
Muons are subatomic particles created when cosmic rays strike the upper atmosphere about $10~\text{km}$ above the Earth's surface (measured in the Earth's reference frame). A muon created in this way travels straight down at a speed of $0.80c$. The **proper** mean lifetime of a muon (measured in the muon's rest frame) is $2.0~\mu\text{s}$. Take $c = 3.0 \times 10^8~\text{m/s}$.

(a) Calculate the Lorentz factor $\gamma$ for the muon. **[1 mark]**

(b) Determine the mean lifetime of the muon as measured by an observer on Earth. **[1 mark]**

(c) Calculate the **mean distance** the muon travels in the Earth frame in one dilated mean lifetime. **[2 marks]**

(d) Without the effects of special relativity, the muon would travel only $480~\text{m}$ in one proper lifetime. Use your answers to explain why a significant number of muons are nevertheless detected at the Earth's surface. **[2 marks]**

(e) In the muon's rest frame, the distance to the Earth's surface is length-contracted. Calculate this contracted distance. **[2 marks]**
