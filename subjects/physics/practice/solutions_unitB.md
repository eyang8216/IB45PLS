The pressure quadruples.

### Solution 11 *(HL)*

**Answer: B — The peak shifts to higher speed and the curve becomes broader.**

At a higher temperature, the Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution:
- The most probable speed increases (peak shifts right)
- The distribution flattens and broadens (more molecules at high speeds)
- The area under the curve remains constant (normalized)

These features arise because:
- v_mp ∝ √T, so peak moves to higher speed
- The exponential factor exp(−mv²/2kT) decays more slowly at higher T, broadening the tail

### Solution 15 [6 marks]

**(a) Explanation of (1−α)S/4 [2 marks]**

The planet intercepts stellar radiation over its cross-sectional area πR². The total incident power is therefore S × πR². ✓ [1]

This power is spread over the entire surface area of the planet, 4πR². The average power per unit area incident on the surface is:

\[
\frac{S \times \pi R^2}{4\pi R^2} = \frac{S}{4}
\]

A fraction α (the albedo) is reflected, so the fraction absorbed is (1 − α). Hence the absorbed power per unit area is (1 − α)S/4. ✓ [1]

**(b) Equilibrium temperature (no atmosphere) [2 marks]**

At radiative equilibrium, absorbed power equals emitted power:

\[
(1 - \alpha)\frac{S}{4} = \sigma T^4
\]

\[
0.75 \times \frac{900}{4} = \sigma T^4
\]

\[
168.75 = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times T^4
\]

\[
T^4 = \frac{168.75}{5.67 \times 10^{-8}} = 2.976 \times 10^9
\]

\[
T = (2.976 \times 10^9)^{1/4}
\]

Using T⁴: 2.976 × 10⁹. Since 233⁴ ≈ (2.33 × 10²)⁴ = 29.5 × 10⁸ = 2.95 × 10⁹:

\[
T \approx 233 \text{ K}
\]

**Answer: 233 K** (−40°C) ✓✓ [2]

*Award [1] for correct equation, [1] for correct temperature.*

New surface temperature:

### Solution 23 [7 marks] *(HL)*

**(a) Initial temperature [1 mark]**

\[
T_1 = \frac{P_1 V_1}{nR} = \frac{(8.0 \times 10^5) \times (5.0 \times 10^{-3})}{2.0 \times 8.31}
= \frac{4000}{16.62} = 240.7 \text{ K} \approx 241 \text{ K}
\]

**Answer: 241 K** ✓ [1]

**(b) Final pressure P₂ [2 marks]**

For an adiabatic process: P₁V₁^γ = P₂V₂^γ

\[
P_2 = P_1 \left(\frac{V_1}{V_2}\right)^{\gamma}
= 8.0 \times 10^5 \times \left(\frac{5.0 \times 10^{-3}}{1.50 \times 10^{-2}}\right)^{5/3}
\]

\[
= 8.0 \times 10^5 \times \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{5/3}
\]

\[
3^{5/3} = \sqrt[3]{3^5} = \sqrt[3]{243} \approx 6.240
\]

\[
\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{5/3} = \frac{1}{6.240} = 0.1603
\]

\[
P_2 = 8.0 \times 10^5 \times 0.1603 = 1.282 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}
\]

**Answer: 1.28 × 10⁵ Pa** ✓✓ [2]

*Award [1] for correct use of PV^γ = constant, [1] for correct numerical answer.*

**(c) Final temperature T₂ [2 marks]**

**Method 1** — Using ideal gas law:

\[
T_2 = \frac{P_2 V_2}{nR} = \frac{(1.282 \times 10^5) \times (1.50 \times 10^{-2})}{2.0 \times 8.31}

\]

**Method 2** — Using TV^(γ−1) = constant:

\[
T_2 = T_1 \left(\frac{V_1}{V_2}\right)^{\gamma - 1} = 241 \times \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{2/3}
\]

\[
3^{2/3} = \sqrt[3]{9} \approx 2.080
\quad\Rightarrow\quad T_2 = \frac{241}{2.080} = 115.9 \text{ K}
\]

**Answer: 116 K** ✓✓ [2]

*Award [1] for correct formula, [1] for correct answer.*

**(d) Work done by the gas [2 marks]**

For an adiabatic process, Q = 0. From the First Law:

\[
\Delta U = Q - W \quad\Rightarrow\quad W = -\Delta U
\]

For a monatomic ideal gas:

\[
\Delta U = \frac{3}{2} nR (T_2 - T_1) = \frac{3}{2} \times 2.0 \times 8.31 \times (115.7 - 240.7)

\[
\Delta U = 3 \times 8.31 \times (-125) = -3116 \text{ J}
\]

\[
W = -\Delta U = +3116 \text{ J}
\]

**Answer: 3.12 × 10³ J** (3120 J) ✓✓ [2]

### Solution 25 [7 marks] *(HL)*

**(b) Total equivalent capacitance [2 marks]**

C_p (6.0 μF) is in series with C₃ (6.0 μF). For series capacitors:

\[
\frac{1}{C_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{C_p} + \frac{1}{C_3} = \frac{1}{6.0} + \frac{1}{6.0} = \frac{2}{6.0}
\]

\[
C_{\text{total}} = \frac{6.0}{2} = 3.0 \text{ μF}
\]

**Answer: 3.0 μF** ✓✓ [2]

*Award [1] for recognizing Cp is in series with C₃, [1] for correct answer.*

**(c) Total charge stored [1 mark]**

Q_{\text{total}} = C_{\text{total}} \times V = (3.0 \times 10^{-6}) \times 12 = 3.6 \times 10^{-5} \text{ C}
\]

**Answer: 36 μC** ✓ [1]

**(d) PD across C₁ [2 marks]**

In a series circuit, the charge on each series element is the same:

\[
Q_p = Q_3 = Q_{\text{total}} = 36 \text{ μC}
\]

The PD across the parallel combination:

\[
V_p = \frac{Q_p}{C_p} = \frac{36 \times 10^{-6}}{6.0 \times 10^{-6}} = 6.0 \text{ V}
\]

Since C₁ is in parallel with C₂, the PD across C₁ equals V_p:

**Answer: 6.0 V** ✓✓ [2]

**(e) Energy stored in C₃ [1 mark]**

The PD across C₃ is: V₃ = V − V_p = 12 − 6.0 = 6.0 V (or Q_total/C₃ = 36/6.0 = 6.0 V).
