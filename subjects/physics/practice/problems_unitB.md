## Paper 2 — Structured Questions

### Question 13 [7 marks]
A 500 W electric heater is used to supply energy to 150 g of ice initially at −15°C. The ice is in an insulated container.

Given:
- Specific heat capacity of ice, c_ice = 2100 J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹
- Specific heat capacity of water, c_water = 4200 J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹
- Specific latent heat of fusion of ice, L_f = 3.34×10⁵ J kg⁻¹

(a) Calculate the energy required to raise the temperature of the ice from −15°C to 0°C. **[1 mark]**

(b) Calculate the energy required to melt all the ice at 0°C. **[1 mark]**

(c) Calculate the total time needed for the ice to completely melt, starting from −15°C. **[2 marks]**

(d) After melting, heating continues. Calculate the additional time needed to raise the water temperature from 0°C to 60°C. **[2 marks]**

(e) State one reason why the actual time measured would differ from the calculated time. **[1 mark]**

### Question 14 [5 marks]

(a) Calculate the total power radiated by the sphere. **[2 marks]**

(b) The sphere is placed inside a vacuum chamber whose walls are maintained at 300 K. Calculate the **net** power radiated by the sphere. **[2 marks]**

### Question 15 [6 marks]

(a) Explain why the average power per unit area absorbed by the planet is given by (1 − α)S/4, where S is the stellar radiation intensity and α is the albedo. **[2 marks]**

### Question 19 [8 marks]

|---------|-------------|
| A → B | Isobaric expansion at P = 4.0×10⁵ Pa from V_A = 2.0×10⁻³ m³ to V_B = 5.0×10⁻³ m³ |
