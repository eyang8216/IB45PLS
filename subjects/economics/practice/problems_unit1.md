# IB Economics SL — Unit 1: Introduction to Economics
## Practice Problems (20 Problems · ~48 Marks)

---

**Topic Coverage:** Scarcity & Choice · Opportunity Cost · Factors of Production & Factor Payments · The Production Possibilities Curve (PPC) · Economic Models, Ceteris Paribus & Equilibrium · Positive vs Normative Statements · Microeconomics vs Macroeconomics

**Problem Types:** Multiple Choice (7) · Short Answer/Definition (7) · Diagram-Based (3) · Calculation (2) · Evaluation (1)

### Multiple Choice

---

**Problem 1.** (2 marks)
Which of the following best defines the economic problem of **scarcity**?

A. There are not enough goods and services to satisfy the wants of the poor.
B. Resources are limited while human wants are unlimited.
C. Governments do not allocate resources efficiently.
D. The price of essential goods continues to rise over time.

---

**Problem 6.** (2 marks)
A government economist states: "The unemployment rate fell from 6.2% to 5.1% last quarter." A second economist responds: "The government should now focus on reducing inflation rather than unemployment."

Which of the following correctly classifies these two statements?

A. Both statements are positive.
B. Both statements are normative.
C. The first is positive; the second is normative.
D. The first is normative; the second is positive.

---

**Problem 8.** (2 marks)
A country discovers large offshore oil reserves and invests the revenue into upgrading its port infrastructure and university research facilities.

Which of the following is the **most likely** long-run effect on the country's PPC?

A. A movement from a point inside the PPC to a point on the PPC.
B. A movement along the existing PPC from consumer goods to capital goods.
C. An outward shift of the PPC.
D. An inward shift of the PPC due to resource depletion.

---

**Problem 10.** (2 marks)
An economist builds a model predicting that a €50 carbon tax per tonne of CO₂ will reduce emissions by 18%, holding all other variables constant.

The phrase "holding all other variables constant" reflects which key concept in economic modelling?

A. Equilibrium analysis
B. The fallacy of composition
C. The ceteris paribus assumption
D. Normative reasoning

---

**Problem 11.** (2 marks)
Which of the following is classified as a **free good**?

A. A prescription drug provided at no charge by a public health service.
B. Air available for breathing at sea level in a rural area.
C. A scholarship place at a university with zero tuition fees.
D. Tap water supplied to households at a subsidised price of $0.02 per litre.

---

**Problem 15.** (2 marks)
The diagram below refers to a country's PPC for capital goods and consumer goods.

*(No diagram is needed — read the description.)*  
Point X lies on the original PPC. Following a period of investment in machinery and education, the economy moves to point Y, which lies on a new PPC that has shifted outward.

This movement from X to Y represents:

A. Actual growth only.
B. Potential growth only.
C. Both actual and potential growth.
D. Neither actual nor potential growth; it represents improved efficiency.

---

**Problem 17.** (2 marks)
A student argues: "Because water is essential for human survival and diamonds are merely decorative, water should always command a higher price than diamonds."

This statement reflects which common economic misconception?

A. Confusing total utility with marginal utility.
B. Confusing scarcity with a lack of usefulness.
C. Confusing a positive statement with a normative statement.
D. Confusing free goods with economic goods.

### Diagram-Based

---

**Problem 4.** (4 marks)
The table below shows the maximum combinations of wheat and steel that an economy can produce per year when all resources are fully and efficiently employed.

| Combination | Wheat (million tonnes) | Steel (million tonnes) |
|---|---|---|
| A | 0 | 500 |
| B | 100 | 450 |
| C | 200 | 350 |
| D | 300 | 200 |
| E | 400 | 0 |

(a) Plot these five combinations on a clearly labelled PPC diagram, with wheat on the horizontal axis and steel on the vertical axis. Join the points. (2 marks)
(b) On the same diagram, plot and label a point **U** that represents unemployment of resources. (1 mark)
(c) On the same diagram, plot and label a point **F** that is currently unattainable but could become attainable in the future. (1 mark)

---

**Problem 14.** (3 marks)
An economy is currently operating at a point inside its PPC for healthcare services and education services. The government then implements a policy that fully employs previously idle doctors and teachers.

(a) Using a PPC diagram with healthcare on the horizontal axis and education on the vertical axis, show:
- A point **P** representing the initial position inside the PPC. (1 mark)
- A point **Q** representing the new position after the policy takes effect, assuming the economy moves to full employment without changing its productive capacity. (1 mark)
(b) Does this movement from P to Q represent **actual growth** or **potential growth**? Justify your answer in one sentence. (1 mark)

### Calculation

---

**Problem 2.** (3 marks)
A software engineer earns a salary of $145,000 per year at her current job. She considers leaving to start her own mobile app development business. She estimates that her first-year business revenue will be $210,000, with explicit costs (office rent, equipment, marketing) of $62,000.

(a) Define **opportunity cost**. (1 mark)
(b) Calculate the opportunity cost to the engineer of starting her own business in the first year. (1 mark)
(c) Calculate the economic profit (or loss) she would earn in the first year. (1 mark)

---

**Problem 12.** (3 marks)
The table below shows a firm's production possibilities for two products per month.

| Product X (units) | 0 | 50 | 100 | 150 | 200 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Product Y (units) | 800 | 750 | 650 | 450 | 0 |

(a) Calculate the opportunity cost of increasing production of Product X from 50 units to 100 units. State your answer in terms of Product Y forgone. (1 mark)
(b) Calculate the opportunity cost of increasing production of Product X from 150 units to 200 units. (1 mark)
(c) Explain what your answers to (a) and (b) reveal about the shape of this PPC. (1 mark)

Use a CLASPP structure in your response (Conclusion, Limitations, Assumptions, Stakeholders, Prioritisation, Proof). Write 150–200 words.
