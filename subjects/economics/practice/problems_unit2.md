### Problem 1 — Demand: Movement vs Shift
**[1 mark]**

The government announces that from next month, all electric vehicle (EV) buyers will receive a £5,000 subsidy. At the same time, a major lithium shortage doubles the price of EV batteries, causing manufacturers to raise EV prices by 12%.

Which of the following correctly describes the **demand-side** effect in the EV market?

A. A rightward shift of the demand curve, because the subsidy reduces the effective price to consumers  
B. A movement along the demand curve to a lower quantity demanded, because EVs become more expensive  
C. Both a rightward shift (from the subsidy) and a leftward shift (from higher prices) of the demand curve  
D. A leftward shift of the demand curve, because consumers anticipate further price rises and delay purchases

Which combination of supply curve shifts results from these two events?

B. Rightward shift from drought (scarcity increases value); leftward shift from tax removal

### Problem 6 — XED: Substitutes vs Complements

### Problem 10 — Market Failure: Externalities

Each MWh produces sulphur dioxide emissions that cause £20 of environmental damage. The marginal social cost is therefore MSC = 50 + 0.5Q. The demand for electricity is given by P = 110 − 0.3Q.

At the **free-market equilibrium** (where MPB = MPC), which of the following is true?

A. Q = 100 MWh, and there is overproduction of 25 MWh relative to the social optimum  
B. Q = 100 MWh, and there is underproduction of 25 MWh relative to the social optimum  
C. Q = 80 MWh, and the market is socially efficient because the externality is internalised  
D. Q = 60 MWh, and there is overproduction of 20 MWh relative to the social optimum
