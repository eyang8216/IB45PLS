# IB AAHL Unit 7: Number, Functions & Graphs — Practice Problems

---

**Problem 1 (Optimization — Fencing)**
A farmer has 200 meters of fencing and wishes to enclose a rectangular field against a long, straight stone wall. The wall forms one side of the rectangle, so fencing is needed only for the other three sides. Let the length of the side parallel to the wall be \(x\) meters and the length of each side perpendicular to the wall be \(y\) meters.
(a) Express \(y\) in terms of \(x\) using the constraint on the total fencing.
(b) Show that the area \(A\) of the field is given by \(A = 100x - \frac{1}{2}x^{2}\).
(c) Determine the dimensions of the field that maximize the area, and find this maximum area.

---

**Problem 2 (Optimization — Open Box)**
An open-topped box is to be made from a rectangular sheet of cardboard measuring 80 cm by 50 cm. Squares of side length \(x\) cm are cut from each corner, and the sides are folded up. Let \(V\) be the volume of the box in \(\text{cm}^{3}\).
(a) Show that \(V = 4x^{3} - 260x^{2} + 4000x\).
(b) Determine the value of \(x\) that maximizes the volume of the box. Give your answer correct to two decimal places.
(c) Calculate the maximum volume.

---

**Problem 3 (Optimization — Cylinder)**
A closed cylindrical can is to be manufactured from sheet metal. The can must hold a volume of \(1000\text{ cm}^{3}\). Let the radius of the base be \(r\text{ cm}\) and the height be \(h\text{ cm}\). The cost of the metal for the curved surface is $0.05 per \(\text{cm}^{2}\), and the cost of the metal for each circular end is $0.08 per \(\text{cm}^{2}\).
(a) Express the height \(h\) in terms of \(r\).
(b) Show that the total cost \(C\) (in dollars) is given by \(C = 0.16\pi r^{2} + \frac{100}{r}\).
(c) Find the radius that minimizes the cost, giving your answer correct to two decimal places. Hence find the minimum cost.

---

**Problem 4 (Binomial Expansion — Specific Coefficient)**
Consider the expansion of \(\left(2x^{3} - \frac{1}{x}\right)^{8}\).
(a) Write down the general term \(T_{r+1}\) in the expansion.
(b) Find the value of \(r\) for which the term is independent of \(x\).
(c) Hence determine the constant term.

---

**Problem 5 (Binomial Expansion — Rational \(n\))**
Use the binomial theorem to expand \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 2x}}\) as a series in ascending powers of \(x\), up to and including the term in \(x^{3}\). State the range of values of \(x\) for which the expansion is valid.

---

**Problem 6 (Binomial Expansion — Coefficient)**
Find the coefficient of \(x^{5}\) in the expansion of \((1 + x)^{3}(1 + 2x)^{6}\).

---

**Problem 7 (Counting — Permutations)**
How many distinct arrangements can be made using all the letters of the word "MISSISSIPPI"? 

---

**Problem 8 (Counting — Permutations with Constraints)**
Five boys and four girls are to sit in a row of nine seats. Find the number of possible seating arrangements if:
(a) there are no restrictions,
(b) the girls must sit together,
(c) the boys and girls must alternate.

---

**Problem 9 (Counting — Combinations)**
A committee of 6 people is to be selected from a group of 8 men and 7 women.
(a) In how many ways can the committee be selected if there are no restrictions?
(b) In how many ways can the committee be selected if it must contain at least 3 women?
(c) In how many ways can the committee be selected if it must contain more men than women?

---

**Problem 10 (Counting — Repeated Arrangements)**
Find the number of distinct arrangements of the letters of the word "PARALLEL" that:
(a) have no restrictions,
(b) begin and end with the letter L,
(c) have all the L's together.

---

**Problem 11 (Polynomial — Remainder Theorem)**
The polynomial \(P(x) = 2x^{3} + ax^{2} + bx - 6\) leaves a remainder of \(-14\) when divided by \((x + 1)\) and a remainder of \(18\) when divided by \((x - 2)\). Find the values of \(a\) and \(b\).

---

**Problem 12 (Polynomial — Factor Theorem)**
Given that \(x = 2\) is a root of the equation \(x^{3} - 7x^{2} + kx - 8 = 0\), find the value of \(k\). Hence factorize the cubic polynomial completely.

---

**Problem 13 (Polynomial — Complex Conjugate Roots)**
A cubic polynomial \(P(x)\) with real coefficients has a root \(x = 1 + 2i\). It is also known that \(P(-1) = 0\) and \(P(0) = -10\). Find \(P(x)\) in the form \(ax^{3} + bx^{2} + cx + d\) where \(a, b, c, d \in \mathbb{R}\).

---

**Problem 14 (Functions — Domain & Range)**
Let \(f(x) = \frac{2x + 1}{x - 3}\) for \(x \neq 3\).
(a) Find the domain and range of \(f\).
(b) Determine the inverse function \(f^{-1}(x)\), and state its domain and range.
(c) Verify that \(f(f^{-1}(x)) = x\).

---

**Problem 15 (Functions — Composite)**
Let \(f(x) = e^{x}\) and \(g(x) = \ln(x - 2)\).
(a) State the largest possible domain for \(g\).
(b) Find the composite function \((f \circ g)(x)\) and simplify.
(c) Find the composite function \((g \circ f)(x)\), stating its domain.
(d) Determine \((g \circ f)(\ln 5)\).

---

**Problem 16 (Functions — Inverse & Domain Restriction)**
Let \(f(x) = x^{2} - 4x + 7\).
(a) Express \(f(x)\) in the form \((x - p)^{2} + q\).
(b) State the minimum value of \(f(x)\) and the value of \(x\) at which it occurs.
(c) Explain why \(f\) does not have an inverse function on \(\mathbb{R}\). Suggest a maximal domain on which \(f^{-1}\) exists, and find \(f^{-1}(x)\) on this restricted domain.

---

**Problem 17 (Transformations — Sequence)**
The graph of \(y = f(x)\) undergoes the following transformations in order:
1. A horizontal stretch by a factor of 3.
2. A reflection in the \(y\)-axis.
3. A translation by the vector \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\).
Find the equation of the transformed graph in terms of \(f\).

---

**Problem 18 (Transformations — Image of a Point)**
A curve has equation \(y = \sqrt{x + 4}\). The curve undergoes two transformations: first, a reflection in the \(x\)-axis, and then a translation by the vector \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}\).
(a) Find the equation of the transformed curve.
(b) The point \(P(0, 2)\) lies on the original curve. Find the coordinates of the image of \(P\) after these transformations.

**Problem 22 (Rational Functions — Asymptotes & Sketching)**
Let \(f(x) = \frac{3x - 2}{x + 1}\) for \(x \neq -1\).
(a) Write down the equations of the vertical and horizontal asymptotes.
(b) Find the coordinates of the points where the graph crosses the coordinate axes.
(c) Sketch the graph of \(y = f(x)\), indicating clearly the asymptotes and intercepts.

---

**Problem 23 (Rational Functions — Inequality)**

---

**Problem 24 (Rational Functions — Advanced Inequality)**

---

**Problem 25 (Multi-part — Functions, Modulus & Transformations)**
A function is defined by \(f(x) = \lvert x^{2} - 4 \rvert\) for \(x \in \mathbb{R}\).
(a) Sketch the graph of \(y = f(x)\), showing clearly any intercepts with the axes and the coordinates of any turning points.
(b) Solve the equation \(f(x) = 5\).
(c) The graph of \(y = f(x)\) is translated by the vector \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\). Write down the equation of the transformed graph, and find the coordinates of the \(y\)-intercept of this transformed graph.
