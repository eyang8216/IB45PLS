# Unit 2: Sequences & Series — Practice Problems

## Problem 1
Determine whether the sequence \(a_n = \frac{2n^2 + 3n}{n^2 - n + 1}\) converges or diverges as \(n \to \infty\). If it converges, state the limit.

## Problem 2
Determine whether the sequence \(b_n = \frac{(-1)^n \cdot n}{n+1}\) converges or diverges as \(n \to \infty\). Justify your answer.

## Problem 3
Find the sum of the infinite geometric series \(12 + 6 + 3 + \frac{3}{2} + \cdots\). State the value of the common ratio and explain why the series converges.

## Problem 4
Express the repeating decimal \(0.\overline{72} = 0.727272\ldots\) as a fraction in lowest terms.

## Problem 5
Consider the series \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{3}{4^n}\). (a) Show that this is a geometric series and identify the first term and common ratio. (b) Determine whether the series converges, and if so, find its sum.

## Problem 6
For the series \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^p}\), state the values of \(p\) for which the series converges. Then determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^{1.5}}\) converges or diverges.

## Problem 7
Use the Integral Test to determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2 + 1}\) converges or diverges.

## Problem 8
Use the Comparison Test to determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2 + 3n}\) converges or diverges.

## Problem 9
Use the Limit Comparison Test to determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n}{n^2 + 1}\) converges or diverges.

## Problem 10
Use the Ratio Test to determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{3^n}{n!}\) converges or diverges.

## Problem 11
Use the Ratio Test to determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n^2}{2^n}\) converges or diverges.

## Problem 12
Determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n}\) converges or diverges. State whether the convergence is absolute or conditional.

## Problem 13
Determine whether \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{\sqrt{n}}\) converges absolutely, converges conditionally, or diverges.

## Problem 14
Find the radius of convergence of the power series \(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{2^n}\).

## Problem 15
Find the radius and interval of convergence of \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(x-3)^n}{n \cdot 2^n}\). Check the endpoints.

## Problem 16
Find the Maclaurin series for \(f(x) = \frac{1}{1+x}\) up to and including the \(x^4\) term.

## Problem 17
Find the Maclaurin series for \(f(x) = \sin x\) up to and including the \(x^5\) term. Write the series in sigma notation.

## Problem 18
Find the Taylor series for \(f(x) = \ln x\) centered at \(x = 1\) up to and including the \((x-1)^3\) term.

## Problem 19
Use the Maclaurin series for \(e^x\) to find the Maclaurin series for \(f(x) = e^{2x}\) up to the \(x^4\) term.

## Problem 20
Find the first four non-zero terms of the Maclaurin series for \(f(x) = \cos(x^2)\).

## Problem 21
Use the Maclaurin series for \(\sin x\) to evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x - x}{x^3}\).

## Problem 22
A function has derivatives \(f(0) = 2\), \(f'(0) = 1\), \(f''(0) = -2\), \(f'''(0) = 6\), and \(f^{(4)}(0) = -24\). Write the Maclaurin polynomial of degree 4 for \(f\).

## Problem 23
Find the Lagrange error bound when approximating \(\sin(0.5)\) using the Maclaurin polynomial of degree 3. Use the fact that the absolute value of all derivatives of \(\sin x\) is at most 1.

## Problem 24
Determine how many terms of the alternating series \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2}\) are needed to approximate its sum with error less than \(0.001\).

## Problem 25
(a) Show that the series \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{2^n}{n!}\) converges. (b) Find the sum of the series. Hint: Consider the Maclaurin series for \(e^x\).
