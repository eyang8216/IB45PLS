# Unit 3: Differential Equations — Practice Problems

## Problem 1
Solve the separable differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = xy$$ subject to the initial condition $$y(0) = 1.$$ Express the solution in the form $$y = f(x).$$

## Problem 2
Solve the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x+1}{y+2}$$ given that $$y(0) = 0.$$ Give your answer as an implicit equation relating $$x$$ and $$y,$$ and determine the domain on which the solution is valid.

## Problem 3
Find the particular solution to the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = e^{x+y}$$ that satisfies $$y(0) = \ln 2.$$ Express $$y$$ explicitly in terms of $$x.$$

## Problem 4
Solve $$\frac{dy}{dx} = y\cos x$$ with the initial condition $$y(0) = 3.$$ Write the solution in the form $$y = f(x).$$

## Problem 5
A differential equation of the form $$\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x, y)$$ has the slope field shown below (imagine a grid where at the point $$(0, 0)$$ the slope is $$0,$$ at $$(1, 1)$$ the slope is approximately $$2,$$ and at $$(-1, -1)$$ the slope is approximately $$2$$). The slope at any point appears to depend only on the ratio $$\frac{y}{x}$$ for $$x \neq 0.$$

(a) Explain why the slope field suggests a homogeneous differential equation of the form $$\frac{dy}{dx} = g\!\left(\frac{y}{x}\right).$$

(b) Given that the slope at $$(1, 2)$$ is $$\frac{5}{4},$$ determine the function $$g.$$

(c) Solve the differential equation you found in part (b).

## Problem 6
Consider the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = x - y.$$ On the axes provided (draw a grid from $$x = -3$$ to $$x = 3$$ and $$y = -3$$ to $$y = 3$$), sketch the slope field. Then sketch the solution curve that passes through the point $$(0, 1).$$

## Problem 7
The slope field for $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y}{1+x^2}$$ is drawn for $$x \geq 0.$$

(a) Describe the behavior of the slope field as $$x \to \infty.$$

(b) Given that $$y(0) = 2,$$ solve the differential equation and determine $$\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty} y(x).$$

(c) Does the limit depend on the initial value $$y(0)?$$ Justify your answer.

## Problem 8
Find the general solution to the first-order linear differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} + 2y = e^x.$$ Clearly identify the integrating factor you use.

## Problem 9
Solve the initial value problem $$\frac{dy}{dx} + y\tan x = \sec x,\quad y(0) = 1,$$ for $$-\frac{\pi}{2} < x < \frac{\pi}{2}.$$

## Problem 10
Find the particular solution to $$x\frac{dy}{dx} + 2y = x^2,\quad x > 0,$$ that satisfies $$y(1) = 1.$$

## Problem 11
Solve the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} - \frac{y}{x} = x^2,\quad x > 0,$$ subject to $$y(1) = 2.$$ Express $$y$$ as a function of $$x.$$

## Problem 12
By using the substitution $$v = \dfrac{y}{x},$$ show that the homogeneous differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x+y}{x}$$ can be reduced to a separable equation in $$v$$ and $$x,$$ and hence find the particular solution satisfying $$y(1) = 1.$$

## Problem 13
Consider the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{2xy},\quad x > 0,\; y > 0.$$

(a) Show that this equation is homogeneous.

(b) Use the substitution $$y = vx$$ to transform the equation into a separable differential equation in $$v$$ and $$x.$$

(c) Hence find the general solution, giving your answer as an implicit equation relating $$x$$ and $$y.$$

## Problem 14
Solve the homogeneous differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y^2 - x^2}{2xy},\quad x > 0,$$ given that $$y(1) = 2.$$ Express the solution in the form $$y = f(x).$$

## Problem 15
A population of rabbits on an island grows according to the logistic differential equation $$\frac{dP}{dt} = kP\!\left(1 - \frac{P}{M}\right),$$ where $$P(t)$$ is the population at time $$t$$ years, $$k = 0.5$$ per year, and the carrying capacity is $$M = 1000.$$ Initially there are $$100$$ rabbits.

(a) Show that the differential equation is separable and solve it to find $$P(t).$$

(b) Determine the time at which the population reaches $$500.$$

(c) Find $$\displaystyle\lim_{t\to\infty} P(t)$$ and interpret this limit in the context of the model.

## Problem 16
A cup of coffee at temperature $$90^\circ\text{C}$$ is placed in a room maintained at a constant temperature of $$20^\circ\text{C}.$$ According to Newton's law of cooling, the rate of change of the temperature $$T(t)$$ of the coffee satisfies $$\frac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - 20),$$ where $$k > 0$$ is a constant and $$t$$ is measured in minutes.

(a) Solve the differential equation to find $$T(t)$$ in terms of $$k.$$

(b) After $$5$$ minutes the temperature of the coffee is $$65^\circ\text{C}.$$ Determine the value of $$k,$$ giving your answer to three significant figures.

(c) How long will it take for the coffee to cool to $$30^\circ\text{C}?$$ Give your answer to the nearest minute.

## Problem 17
A tank initially contains $$200$$ liters of pure water. A salt solution with a concentration of $$0.3$$ kilograms per liter flows into the tank at a rate of $$4$$ liters per minute. The well-stirred mixture flows out at the same rate of $$4$$ liters per minute. Let $$S(t)$$ be the amount of salt (in kilograms) in the tank at time $$t$$ minutes.

(a) Show that $$S(t)$$ satisfies the differential equation $$\frac{dS}{dt} = 1.2 - \frac{S}{50}.$$

(b) Solve the differential equation to find $$S(t),$$ given that $$S(0) = 0.$$

(c) Determine the limiting amount of salt in the tank as $$t \to \infty,$$ and explain what this represents physically.

## Problem 18
Show that the function $$y = \dfrac{\sin x}{x},\; x > 0,$$ is a solution of the differential equation $$x\frac{dy}{dx} + y = \cos x.$$

## Problem 19
Consider the family of curves given by $$x^2 - y^2 = Cx,$$ where $$C$$ is an arbitrary constant.

(a) Differentiate implicitly to find $$\frac{dy}{dx}$$ in terms of $$x$$ and $$y$$ for this family.

(b) Show that every member of this family satisfies the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{2xy}.$$

(c) Verify that the particular curve with $$C = 3$$ passes through the point $$(1, 0)$$ and that the differential equation is indeed satisfied at this point.

## Problem 20
A bacterial culture grows at a rate proportional to its size. Initially the culture contains $$500$$ bacteria, and after $$3$$ hours it contains $$2000$$ bacteria.

(a) Write down and solve the differential equation governing the population $$N(t).$$

(b) Find the population after $$6$$ hours.

(c) Determine the doubling time of the bacteria.

## Problem 21
A radioactive substance decays at a rate proportional to the amount present. Initially there are $$80$$ milligrams of the substance, and after $$10$$ days only $$50$$ milligrams remain.

(a) Find the half-life of the substance, giving your answer to the nearest day.

(b) Write a formula for the mass $$m(t)$$ remaining after $$t$$ days.

(c) After how many days will only $$10$$ milligrams remain?

## Problem 22
A tank contains $$100$$ liters of brine with $$10$$ kilograms of dissolved salt. Pure water enters the tank at a rate of $$5$$ liters per minute, and the well-stirred mixture leaves at the same rate.

(a) Write a differential equation for the amount of salt $$A(t)$$ in the tank at time $$t.$$

(b) Solve the differential equation and find $$A(t).$$

(c) How much salt remains after $$20$$ minutes? Give your answer to two decimal places.

## Problem 23
A metal bar is heated to $$200^\circ\text{C}$$ and then placed in a large room kept at $$25^\circ\text{C}.$$ The temperature of the bar drops to $$150^\circ\text{C}$$ in $$4$$ minutes. Assuming Newton's law of cooling applies, find the temperature of the bar after $$10$$ minutes.

## Problem 24
Solve the initial value problem $$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\sin x}{y},\quad y(0) = 2,$$ for $$x \geq 0.$$ Determine the largest interval on which the solution is defined.

## Problem 25
Consider the differential equation $$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)$$ where $$P(x) = \frac{2}{x}$$ and $$Q(x) = \frac{\ln x}{x},\; x > 0.$$

(a) Find the integrating factor $$\mu(x).$$

(b) Hence find the general solution for $$y(x).$$

(c) Determine the particular solution that satisfies $$\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0^+} y(x) = 0,$$ or explain why no such solution exists.
