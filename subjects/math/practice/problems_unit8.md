# IB AAHL Unit 8: Trigonometry & Geometry — Practice Problems

---

**Problem 1 (Compound Angle — Exact Value)**
Use the compound angle formula to find the exact value of \(\cos 75^{\circ}\), expressing your answer in the form \(\frac{\sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}}{c}\) where \(a, b, c \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}\).

---

**Problem 2 (Double Angle — Exact Value)**
Given that \(\sin \theta = \frac{3}{5}\) and \(0 < \theta < \frac{\pi}{2}\), find the exact values of:
(a) \(\sin 2\theta\),
(b) \(\cos 2\theta\),
(c) \(\tan 2\theta\).

---

**Problem 3 (Compound Angle — Simplification)**
Simplify \(\sin(A + B)\cos B - \cos(A + B)\sin B\) and hence write the expression in its simplest form.

---

**Problem 4 (Proving Identity)**
Prove the identity:

\[
\frac{1 - \cos 2\theta}{\sin 2\theta} = \tan \theta.
\]

State any restrictions on \(\theta\).

---

**Problem 5 (Proving Identity)**
Prove the identity:

\[
\frac{\sin 3x}{\sin x} - \frac{\cos 3x}{\cos x} = 2.
\]

---

**Problem 6 (Solving — Basic)**
Solve the equation \(2\sin x = 1\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 7 (Solving — Quadratic in \(\sin\))**
Solve the equation \(2\sin^{2} x - 3\sin x + 1 = 0\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 8 (Solving — Quadratic in \(\cos\))**
Solve the equation \(2\cos^{2} x + 3\cos x - 2 = 0\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 9 (Solving — Using Identity)**
Solve the equation \(\cos 2x = \sin x\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 10 (Solving — Multiple Angles)**
Solve the equation \(\sin 3x = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 11 (Solving — Multiple Angles)**
Solve the equation \(\tan 2x = -\sqrt{3}\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 12 (Solving — Using Compound Identity)**
Solve the equation \(\sin x + \cos x = 1\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).
Hint: Express the left-hand side in the form \(R\sin(x + \alpha)\).

---

**Problem 13 (Reciprocal Trig — Equation with \(\sec\))**
Solve the equation \(\sec^{2} x - 3\sec x + 2 = 0\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 14 (Reciprocal Trig — Equation with \(\csc\) and \(\cot\))**
Solve the equation \(\csc x = 2\cot x\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\).

---

**Problem 15 (Inverse Trig — Evaluation)**
Evaluate each of the following, giving exact answers:
(a) \(\arcsin\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\),
(b) \(\arccos\left(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)\),
(c) \(\arctan(-1)\),
(d) \(\cos\left(\arcsin\left(\frac{3}{5}\right)\right)\).

---

**Problem 16 (Inverse Trig — Composition)**
Find the exact value of \(\tan\left(\arcsin\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) + \arccos\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)\right)\). Express your answer in simplest radical form.

---

**Problem 17 (Radians — Arc Length & Sector Area)**
A circle has radius 12 cm. A sector of this circle subtends an angle of \(\frac{2\pi}{3}\) radians at the centre.
(a) Find the length of the arc of the sector.
(b) Find the area of the sector.
(c) Find the area of the segment (the region between the chord and the arc).

---

**Problem 18 (Radians — Arc Length)**
A pendulum of length 40 cm swings through an angle of \(18^{\circ}\). Find, to two decimal places, the distance traveled by the tip of the pendulum in one swing from one extreme to the other.

---

**Problem 19 (Radians — Segment Area)**
A chord of a circle of radius 10 cm subtends an angle of \(\frac{\pi}{3}\) radians at the centre. Find the area of the minor segment.

---

**Problem 20 (Volume of Revolution — Disk Method, \(x\)-axis)**
The region bounded by the curve \(y = \sqrt{x}\), the \(x\)-axis, and the lines \(x = 1\) and \(x = 4\) is rotated completely about the \(x\)-axis. Find the volume of the solid generated.

---

**Problem 21 (Volume of Revolution — Disk Method, \(y\)-axis)**
The region bounded by the curve \(y = x^{2}\), the \(y\)-axis, and the line \(y = 4\) is rotated completely about the \(y\)-axis. Find the volume of the solid generated.

---

**Problem 22 (Volume of Revolution — Washer Method)**
The region bounded by the curves \(y = x^{2}\) and \(y = 2x\) is rotated completely about the \(x\)-axis. Find the volume of the solid generated.

---

**Problem 23 (Trig + Calculus — Connected)**
A curve is defined by \(y = \sin x\) for \(0 \leq x \leq \pi\). The region bounded by this curve and the \(x\)-axis is rotated completely about the \(x\)-axis.
(a) Set up the integral for the volume of revolution.
(b) Use the identity \(\sin^{2} x = \frac{1 - \cos 2x}{2}\) to evaluate the integral.
(c) Hence find the exact volume.

---

**Problem 24 (Trig + Calculus — Connected)**
Consider the function \(f(x) = x\cos x\) for \(0 \leq x \leq \frac{\pi}{2}\).
(a) Find \(f'(x)\).
(b) Determine the equation of the tangent to the curve \(y = f(x)\) at the point where \(x = \frac{\pi}{3}\).
(c) The region bounded by \(y = f(x)\), the \(x\)-axis, and the line \(x = \frac{\pi}{2}\) is rotated about the \(x\)-axis. Set up (but do not evaluate) the integral for the volume generated.

---

**Problem 25 (Multi-part — Trig Equations, Radians & Transformations)**
A function is defined by \(f(x) = 3\sin\left(2x - \frac{\pi}{3}\right) + 1\) for \(x \in \mathbb{R}\).
(a) State the amplitude, period, and phase shift of \(f\).
(b) Find all solutions to the equation \(f(x) = 2.5\) for \(x \in [0, 2\pi]\). Give your answers correct to three significant figures.
(c) The graph of \(y = f(x)\) is translated so that its minimum point is mapped to the origin. Describe this translation and write the equation of the transformed function.
