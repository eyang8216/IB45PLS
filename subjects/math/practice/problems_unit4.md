# Unit 4: Complex Numbers — Practice Problems

## Problem 1
Let $$z = 3 - 2i$$ and $$w = -1 + 5i.$$ Compute each of the following in the Cartesian form $$a + bi:$$

(a) $$z + w$$

(b) $$z - w$$

(c) $$zw$$

(d) $$z^2$$

## Problem 2
Express the complex number $$\frac{3 + 2i}{1 - 4i}$$ in the Cartesian form $$a + bi,$$ where $$a, b \in \mathbb{R}.$$

## Problem 3
Given $$z = 4 - 3i,$$ find:

(a) The complex conjugate $$\overline{z}$$

(b) The modulus $$|z|$$

(c) The product $$z\overline{z}$$

(d) The real and imaginary parts of $$\frac{1}{z}$$

## Problem 4
Find the real numbers $$x$$ and $$y$$ such that $$(x + yi)(2 + i) = 5 - i.$$

## Problem 5
For the complex number $$z = -1 + i\sqrt{3},$$ find:

(a) The modulus $$|z|$$

(b) The argument $$\arg z,$$ giving your answer in radians in the interval $$(-\pi, \pi]$$

(c) Hence write $$z$$ in polar form $$r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$$

(d) Write $$z$$ in exponential form $$re^{i\theta}$$

## Problem 6
Sketch the locus of points $$z$$ in the Argand diagram that satisfy $$|z| = 4.$$ Describe the locus geometrically.

## Problem 7
Sketch and describe the locus of points $$z$$ in the complex plane that satisfy $$|z - 2| = |z + 2i|.$$

## Problem 8
Sketch the locus of points $$z$$ that satisfy $$\arg(z - 1 - i) = \frac{\pi}{4}.$$ Give the Cartesian equation of this locus.

## Problem 9
A point $$z$$ in the complex plane satisfies both $$|z - 3| = 2$$ and $$|z| = |z - 6|.$$

(a) Describe each locus geometrically.

(b) Find all values of $$z$$ that satisfy both conditions, giving your answers in Cartesian form.

## Problem 10
Convert the complex number $$z = -\sqrt{3} + i$$ into:

(a) Polar form $$r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$$

(b) Exponential form $$re^{i\theta}$$

## Problem 11
A complex number $$z$$ has modulus $$2$$ and argument $$\frac{2\pi}{3}.$$ Express $$z$$ in Cartesian form $$a + bi.$$

## Problem 12
Write the complex number $$z = -2 - 2i\sqrt{3}$$ in exponential form $$re^{i\theta},$$ where $$r > 0$$ and $$-\pi < \theta \leq \pi.$$

## Problem 13
Use De Moivre's theorem to compute $$(1 + i\sqrt{3})^6.$$ Give your answer in Cartesian form.

## Problem 14
Use De Moivre's theorem to express $$\cos 3\theta$$ in terms of $$\cos\theta$$ and $$\sin 4\theta$$ in terms of $$\sin\theta$$ and $$\cos\theta.$$

## Problem 15

(a) By considering $$(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)^5,$$ use De Moivre's theorem to show that
$$\cos 5\theta = 16\cos^5\theta - 20\cos^3\theta + 5\cos\theta.$$

(b) Hence find all solutions to the equation $$16c^5 - 20c^3 + 5c = 0$$ for $$c \in \mathbb{R}.$$

## Problem 16
Find the three cube roots of unity. That is, solve the equation $$z^3 = 1.$$ Give your answers in both polar and Cartesian forms, and plot them on an Argand diagram.

## Problem 17
Solve the equation $$z^4 = -16.$$ Express all four roots in Cartesian form $$a + bi.$$

## Problem 18
Find all five distinct solutions to the equation $$z^5 = 1 - i.$$ Give your answers in polar form $$r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta),$$ with $$r > 0$$ and arguments in $$(-\pi, \pi].$$

## Problem 19
Given that $$1 + 2i$$ is a root of the polynomial equation $$z^3 - z^2 + 3z + 5 = 0,$$ find the remaining two roots.

## Problem 20
The polynomial $$P(z) = z^4 - 2z^3 + 5z^2 - 8z + 4$$ has a complex root $$1 + i.$$

(a) Write down another complex root of $$P(z).$$

(b) Hence factorize $$P(z)$$ completely over the complex numbers.

(c) State the factorization of $$P(z)$$ over the real numbers.

## Problem 21
The complex numbers $$z_1 = 2 + i$$ and $$z_2 = -1 + 3i$$ represent two vertices of a triangle in the Argand diagram. The third vertex is the origin $$O.$$

(a) Plot the triangle on an Argand diagram.

(b) Find the lengths of all three sides of the triangle.

(c) Determine whether the triangle is right-angled, giving a reason for your answer.

## Problem 22
Let $$z = 2 + 2i.$$

(a) Find $$|z|$$ and $$\arg z.$$

(b) The complex number $$w$$ is obtained by rotating $$z$$ about the origin by an angle of $$\frac{\pi}{3}$$ radians counterclockwise and then doubling its distance from the origin. Express $$w$$ in Cartesian form.

(c) Find $$|w|$$ and verify that it equals $$2|z|.$$

## Problem 23
Describe and sketch the locus of points $$z$$ in the complex plane that satisfy $$|z - i| = 2|z + i|.$$

(a) Rewrite the condition as a Cartesian equation in $$x$$ and $$y.$$

(b) Identify the geometric shape of the locus.

(c) Find the center and radius of this locus.

## Problem 24
Consider the complex numbers $$z = e^{i\theta}$$ and $$w = e^{i\phi},$$ where $$\theta, \phi \in \mathbb{R}.$$

(a) Show that $$\displaystyle\frac{z + w}{z - w} = i\cot\!\left(\frac{\theta - \phi}{2}\right),$$ provided $$z \neq w.$$

(b) Hence or otherwise, find all values of $$\theta \in [0, 2\pi)$$ for which $$\displaystyle\left|\frac{e^{i\theta} + 1}{e^{i\theta} - 1}\right| = 1.$$

## Problem 25
Let $$\omega = e^{2\pi i/5}$$ be a primitive fifth root of unity.

(a) Show that $$1 + \omega + \omega^2 + \omega^3 + \omega^4 = 0.$$

(b) By considering the real part of this sum, show that $$\cos\frac{2\pi}{5} + \cos\frac{4\pi}{5} = -\frac{1}{2}.$$

(c) Hence, using the identity $$\cos 2\theta = 2\cos^2\theta - 1,$$ find the exact value of $$\cos\frac{2\pi}{5}.$$
