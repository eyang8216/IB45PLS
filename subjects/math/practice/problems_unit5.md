# IB AAHL Unit 5: Vectors & Matrices — Practice Problems

---

## Problem 1
Given the vectors **a** = 3**i** − 2**j** + **k** and **b** = −**i** + 4**j** + 2**k**, find:
- (a) the dot product **a** · **b**;
- (b) the angle between **a** and **b**, correct to the nearest 0.1°.

---

## Problem 2
Let **u** = 2**i** + **j** − 3**k** and **v** = **i** − 2**j** + 4**k**.
- (a) Compute the cross product **u** × **v**.
- (b) Verify that **u** × **v** is orthogonal to both **u** and **v**.
- (c) Find a unit vector perpendicular to both **u** and **v**.

---

## Problem 3
Find the area of the triangle with vertices \(A(1, 2, 3)\), \(B(4, 0, −1)\), and \(C(2, 5, 1)\).

---

## Problem 4
A line \(L_1\) passes through the point \(P(2, −1, 5)\) and is parallel to the vector **d** = 3**i** − **j** + 2**k**.
- (a) Write a vector equation for \(L_1\).
- (b) Write parametric equations for \(L_1\).
- (c) Write the Cartesian equations for \(L_1\).

## Problem 5
A line \(L_2\) passes through the points \(A(1, 3, −2)\) and \(B(5, −1, 4)\). Find:
- (a) a direction vector for \(L_2\);
- (b) parametric equations for \(L_2\);
- (c) the Cartesian equations for \(L_2\).

## Problem 6
Determine whether the lines

\[
L_1: \mathbf{r} = (1, 0, 2) + \lambda(2, −1, 3),\quad \lambda \in \mathbb{R}
\]
\[
L_2: \mathbf{r} = (3, −1, 5) + \mu(−4, 2, −6),\quad \mu \in \mathbb{R}
\]

are the same line, parallel and distinct, intersecting, or skew. Justify your answer.

---

## Problem 7
Two lines are given by

\[
L_1: \frac{x − 1}{2} = \frac{y + 3}{−1} = \frac{z − 2}{4},\qquad
L_2: \frac{x − 4}{−1} = \frac{y − 1}{2} = \frac{z + 1}{2}.
\]

- (a) Verify that \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) are not parallel.
- (b) Show that \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) intersect, and find their point of intersection.

---

## Problem 8
Find the point of intersection of the line

\[
\mathbf{r} = (2, −1, 3) + t(1, 2, −1),\quad t \in \mathbb{R}
\]

and the plane \(2x − y + 3z = 10\).

---

## Problem 9
Two planes are defined by

\[
\Pi_1: x + 2y − z = 4,\qquad \Pi_2: 2x − y + 3z = 1.
\]

- (a) Find a direction vector for the line of intersection of \(\Pi_1\) and \(\Pi_2\).
- (b) Find a point on the line of intersection.
- (c) Hence write a vector equation for the line of intersection.

---

## Problem 10
Find the distance from the point \(P(3, −2, 4)\) to the plane \(2x + y − 2z = 8\).

## Problem 11
Let \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & −1 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ −2 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\). Compute:
- (a) \(A + B\);
- (b) \(AB\);
- (c) \(BA\);
- (d) Verify that \(AB \neq BA\) in general.

## Problem 12

- (b) Compute \(DC\).
- (c) State the dimensions of each product.

## Problem 17
Use Gaussian elimination to solve the system:

\[
\begin{cases}
x + 2y + z = 5 \\
2x − y + 3z = 1 \\
−x + 4y − z = 7
\end{cases}
\]

---

## Problem 18
Use row reduction to solve the system:

\[
\begin{cases}
x + y + z = 2 \\
2x − y + 3z = 5 \\
3x + 0y + 4z = 7
\end{cases}
\]

Determine whether the system has a unique solution, infinitely many solutions, or no solution.

## Problem 20
- (a) Find the value(s) of \(k\) for which the vectors **a** = (1, k, 2) and **b** = (k, 3, −1) are orthogonal.
- (b) For the value(s) of \(k\) found in part (a), compute **a** × **b**.
- (c) Interpret the geometric meaning of the cross product when the vectors are orthogonal.

---

## Problem 21
Let \(M\) be the matrix \(\begin{pmatrix} a & 2 \\ 3 & a \end{pmatrix}\).

- (a) Find \(\det(M)\) in terms of \(a\).
- (b) Find the values of \(a\) for which \(M\) is singular.
- (c) For \(a = 1\), compute \(M^{-1}\) and verify that \(M M^{-1} = I\).

---

## Problem 22
A plane \(\Pi\) passes through the three points \(A(1, 0, 2)\), \(B(3, −1, 4)\), and \(C(0, 2, −1)\).

- (a) Find two direction vectors lying in the plane.
- (b) Hence find a normal vector to the plane.
- (c) Write the Cartesian equation of the plane.
- (d) Find the distance from the origin to the plane.

---

## Problem 23
Let **p** = 2**i** + **j** − **k**, **q** = **i** − 3**j** + 2**k**, and **r** = 4**i** − **j** + 3**k**.

- (a) Compute (**p** × **q**) · **r** (the scalar triple product).
- (b) Hence find the volume of the parallelepiped defined by vectors **p**, **q**, and **r**.
- (c) What does it mean geometrically if the scalar triple product is zero?

---

## Problem 24
Consider the augmented matrix

\[
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & −1 & 2 & | & 4 \\
2 & 1 & −1 & | & 1 \\
1 & −4 & 7 & | & k
\end{pmatrix}
\]

- (a) Reduce the matrix to row-echelon form.
- (b) Determine the value of \(k\) for which the system has infinitely many solutions.
- (c) Determine the value of \(k\) for which the system has no solution.

- (a) Show that \(L\) is not parallel to \(\Pi\).
- (b) Find the point where \(L\) intersects \(\Pi\).
- (c) Find the acute angle between \(L\) and \(\Pi\), correct to the nearest 0.1°.
