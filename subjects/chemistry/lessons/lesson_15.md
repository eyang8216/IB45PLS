## 1. Hess's Law: Why the Path Does Not Matter

### What Does Hess's Law Say?

Hess's Law states that the enthalpy change for a chemical reaction is the same regardless of whether the reaction happens in one step or in a series of steps, as long as the starting materials (the initial state) and the final products (the final state) are the same in every case.

This can be written more formally: **О”H for a reaction depends only on the initial and final states, not on the route taken between them.**

### What Does "Route" Mean?

Imagine you want to travel from New York to Los Angeles. You could fly directly. Or you could fly from New York to Chicago, then from Chicago to Denver, then from Denver to Los Angeles. The distance you travel is different in each case, but you start in New York and end in Los Angeles either way.

In chemistry, the "route" is the sequence of reaction steps. The overall enthalpy change О”H is like the straight-line distance between the starting point and the ending point вҖ” it does not depend on how you got there. The individual О”H values for each step add up to the same total no matter which steps you choose.

### Why Is This Useful?

Some reactions are impossible to measure directly in a calorimeter. For example, you cannot simply put carbon and hydrogen in a calorimeter and measure the enthalpy of formation of ethane directly вҖ” the reaction between C(s) and HвӮӮ(g) does not happen in a simple, clean way. But if you can find a route from carbon and hydrogen to ethane using reactions whose О”H values are known, you can calculate the desired О”H without ever measuring it.

### Enthalpy Is a State Function

The phrase "state function" means a quantity whose value depends only on the current state of the system, not on how the system got there. Your altitude above sea level is a state function вҖ” if you climb a mountain, your final altitude depends only on where you are standing, not on whether you took the steep route or the gentle route. Enthalpy is a state function in exactly the same way.

### Common Misconception

Some students think Hess's Law is a special rule that only applies in certain circumstances. But Hess's Law is simply a consequence of the First Law of Thermodynamics (energy is conserved) and the fact that enthalpy is a state function. It always applies. There are no exceptions.

## 2. Method 1: Calculating О”H Using Standard Enthalpies of Formation (О”H_fвҰө)

### The Logic of the Formation Method

Imagine you have a reaction: Reactants вҶ’ Products. One way to go from reactants to products is directly. Another way is to first "un-form" all the reactants вҖ” break them down into their constituent elements вҖ” and then "form" the products from those same elements.

Un-forming a compound is the reverse of its formation reaction, so the enthalpy change is the negative of О”H_fвҰө. Forming a product from elements has an enthalpy change equal to О”H_fвҰө of that product.

Since both routes must have the same total О”H (Hess's Law), we can write:

**О”H_reaction = ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants)**

The symbol ОЈ (capital Greek letter sigma) means "the sum of." You take each product's О”H_fвҰө, multiply it by the number of moles of that product in the balanced equation (its coefficient), and add them all up. Then you do the same for the reactants. Then you subtract the reactants sum from the products sum.

### Why This Formula Has a Minus Sign

The minus sign appears because the formation enthalpies of reactants are being "reversed" in the enthalpy cycle. Going from reactants to elements is the opposite of forming the reactants from elements, so we subtract instead of adding.

### What to Do with Elements in the Equation

If a reactant or product is an element in its standard state, its О”H_fвҰө is zero. This is the rule we learned in Lesson 14. For example, in the combustion of methane:

CHвӮ„(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l)

OвӮӮ(g) is an element, so О”H_fвҰө[OвӮӮ(g)] = 0. CHвӮ„ is a compound, so its О”H_fвҰө is not zero. COвӮӮ and HвӮӮO are compounds, so their О”H_fвҰө values are not zero.

### Worked Example 1: Combustion of Methane

**Problem:** Calculate the standard enthalpy change for the complete combustion of methane: CHвӮ„(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l). Use the following standard enthalpies of formation:
- О”H_fвҰө[CHвӮ„(g)] = вҲ’75 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_fвҰө[COвӮӮ(g)] = вҲ’394 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_fвҰө[HвӮӮO(l)] = вҲ’286 kJ molвҒ»В№

**Strategy:** We apply the formula О”H = ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants). For each substance, we multiply its О”H_fвҰө by its coefficient from the balanced equation. OвӮӮ is an element so its О”H_fвҰө is zero.

**Step 1 вҖ” Sum of О”H_fвҰө for products:**
The products are COвӮӮ(g) with coefficient 1, and HвӮӮO(l) with coefficient 2.
ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) = 1 Г— (вҲ’394) + 2 Г— (вҲ’286)
= вҲ’394 + (вҲ’572)
= вҲ’966 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 2 вҖ” Sum of О”H_fвҰө for reactants:**
The reactants are CHвӮ„(g) with coefficient 1, and OвӮӮ(g) with coefficient 2.
ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants) = 1 Г— (вҲ’75) + 2 Г— (0)
= вҲ’75 + 0
= вҲ’75 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:**
О”H = ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants)
= вҲ’966 вҲ’ (вҲ’75)
= вҲ’966 + 75
= вҲ’891 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Why this makes sense:** Combustion of methane is known to be highly exothermic, releasing a large amount of energy. A value of вҲ’891 kJ molвҒ»В№ confirms this. The negative sign is correct.

### Worked Example 2: Formation Method with More Complex Stoichiometry

**Problem:** Calculate О”H for the reaction: 2Al(s) + FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s) вҶ’ AlвӮӮOвӮғ(s) + 2Fe(s). The standard enthalpies of formation are:
- О”H_fвҰө[FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s)] = вҲ’824 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_fвҰө[AlвӮӮOвӮғ(s)] = вҲ’1676 kJ molвҒ»В№

**Strategy:** Both Al(s) and Fe(s) are elements in their standard states, so their О”H_fвҰө values are zero. We only need to consider the two compounds.

**Step 1 вҖ” Products:**
ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) = 1 Г— (вҲ’1676) + 2 Г— (0) = вҲ’1676 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 2 вҖ” Reactants:**
ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants) = 2 Г— (0) + 1 Г— (вҲ’824) = вҲ’824 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:**
О”H = вҲ’1676 вҲ’ (вҲ’824) = вҲ’1676 + 824 = вҲ’852 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Why this makes sense:** This is the thermite reaction, famous for being extremely exothermic вҖ” hot enough to melt iron. The large negative О”H confirms this.

## 3. Method 2: Calculating О”H Using Standard Enthalpies of Combustion (О”H_cвҰө)

### The Logic of the Combustion Method

The formation method uses the idea of going "down to elements" and back up. The combustion method uses the idea of going "up to combustion products" (COвӮӮ, HвӮӮO, etc.) and back.

Imagine that both the reactants and the products can be burned in oxygen to produce the same set of combustion products (COвӮӮ, HвӮӮO, SOвӮӮ, etc.). One route from reactants to products is direct. Another route is: burn the reactants to their combustion products, and then "un-burn" those combustion products to form the reaction products.

Un-burning is the reverse of combustion, so the enthalpy change is the negative of О”H_cвҰө. This leads to a formula that is similar to the formation formula but with the order reversed:

**О”H_reaction = ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products)**

Notice carefully: for the combustion method, we subtract the PRODUCTS from the REACTANTS. This is the opposite of the formation method. Many students get the sign wrong by using the formation formula when they should be using the combustion formula. If you always remember "combustion = reactants minus products," you will avoid this error.

### When Is the Combustion Method Used?

The combustion method is most useful for reactions involving organic compounds (hydrocarbons, alcohols, carboxylic acids, etc.), because the enthalpies of combustion of organic compounds are easy to measure experimentally and are widely tabulated. Enthalpies of formation for organic compounds are sometimes harder to find.

Also, if you are given О”H_cвҰө values for all the species in a reaction, use the combustion method. If you are given О”H_fвҰө values, use the formation method. Mixing methods within one calculation is a recipe for errors.

### What about Water?

The О”H_cвҰө of HвӮӮO(l) is zero, because water is already a combustion product вҖ” it cannot be burned further. Similarly, the О”H_cвҰө of COвӮӮ(g) is zero. This makes sense: you cannot burn ash.

### Worked Example 3: Hydrogenation of Ethene

**Problem:** Calculate the enthalpy change for the hydrogenation of ethene: CвӮӮHвӮ„(g) + HвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ CвӮӮHвӮҶ(g). Use the following standard enthalpies of combustion:
- О”H_cвҰө[CвӮӮHвӮ„(g)] = вҲ’1411 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_cвҰө[HвӮӮ(g)] = вҲ’286 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_cвҰө[CвӮӮHвӮҶ(g)] = вҲ’1560 kJ molвҒ»В№

**Strategy:** We use the combustion formula: О”H = ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products).

**Step 1 вҖ” Sum of О”H_cвҰө for reactants:**
ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants) = 1 Г— (вҲ’1411) + 1 Г— (вҲ’286) = вҲ’1697 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 2 вҖ” Sum of О”H_cвҰө for products:**
ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products) = 1 Г— (вҲ’1560) = вҲ’1560 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:**
О”H = вҲ’1697 вҲ’ (вҲ’1560) = вҲ’1697 + 1560 = вҲ’137 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Why this makes sense:** Hydrogenation reactions (adding HвӮӮ across a double bond) are typically exothermic because a weaker ПҖ bond is replaced by a stronger Пғ bond. The negative О”H is consistent with this expectation.

### Worked Example 4: Esterification

**Problem:** Calculate О”H for the esterification reaction: CHвӮғCOOH(l) + CвӮӮHвӮ…OH(l) вҶ’ CHвӮғCOOCвӮӮHвӮ…(l) + HвӮӮO(l). The standard enthalpies of combustion are:
- О”H_cвҰө[CHвӮғCOOH(l)] = вҲ’875 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_cвҰө[CвӮӮHвӮ…OH(l)] = вҲ’1367 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_cвҰө[CHвӮғCOOCвӮӮHвӮ…(l)] = вҲ’2250 kJ molвҒ»В№
- О”H_cвҰө[HвӮӮO(l)] = 0 kJ molвҒ»В№

**Strategy:** Water's О”H_cвҰө is zero. Apply the combustion formula.

**Step 1 вҖ” Reactants:**
ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants) = (вҲ’875) + (вҲ’1367) = вҲ’2242 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 2 вҖ” Products:**
ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products) = (вҲ’2250) + (0) = вҲ’2250 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:**
О”H = вҲ’2242 вҲ’ (вҲ’2250) = вҲ’2242 + 2250 = +8 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Why this makes sense:** Esterification reactions typically have very small О”H values, often close to zero. The value of +8 kJ molвҒ»В№ means this reaction is very slightly endothermic, which is entirely plausible.

## 4. Method 3: Manipulating and Combining Given Equations

### The Puzzle Approach

Sometimes you are given two or three reactions with their О”H values and asked to find О”H for a target reaction. The target reaction does not appear directly in the given data, but you can construct it by combining the given reactions вҖ” flipping them, multiplying them, and adding them together.

This method is sometimes called the "thermochemical equation method" or the "Hess cycle puzzle method." It is the most direct application of Hess's Law: if you can build the target equation from given equations, the О”H for the target is the sum of the О”H values of the equations you combined, with each О”H adjusted for any flipping or multiplying you did.

### Three Allowed Operations

There are exactly three things you are allowed to do to a thermochemical equation:

**Operation 1: Flip (reverse) an equation.**
If you reverse a reaction, you must change the sign of its О”H.
Example: If C(s) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g) has О”H = вҲ’394 kJ, then the reverse, COвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ C(s) + OвӮӮ(g), has О”H = +394 kJ.

**Operation 2: Multiply an equation by a number.**
If you multiply all coefficients in an equation by n, you must multiply О”H by n as well.
Example: If HвӮӮ(g) + ВҪOвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ HвӮӮO(l) has О”H = вҲ’286 kJ, then multiplying by 2 gives 2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 2HвӮӮO(l) with О”H = вҲ’572 kJ.

**Operation 3: Add equations together.**
If you write two (or more) equations and add them, the О”H of the combined equation is the sum of the individual О”H values. When adding equations, identical species appearing on both the left and right sides cancel out, just like in algebra.

### A Systematic Approach

When faced with a "combine equations" problem, follow these steps every time:

1. Write down the target equation.
2. Look at the first substance in the target equation. Find which given equation contains that substance on the correct side. If it is on the wrong side, flip that equation (and change the sign of its О”H).
3. If the coefficient of that substance in the given equation does not match the target, multiply the entire equation (and its О”H) by the appropriate factor.
4. Repeat for the next substance, always making sure substances you do not want in the final equation will cancel when you add everything up.
5. Add all the manipulated equations. Cancel identical species on both sides.
6. Verify that the resulting equation exactly matches the target equation.
7. Add up all the manipulated О”H values to get the answer.

### Worked Example 5: Iron Extraction

**Problem:** Calculate О”H for the reaction: FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s) + 3CO(g) вҶ’ 2Fe(s) + 3COвӮӮ(g). This is the key reaction in a blast furnace for extracting iron. Use the following data:
- Equation (1): 2Fe(s) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s), О”H = вҲ’824 kJ molвҒ»В№
- Equation (2): C(s) + ВҪOвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ CO(g), О”H = вҲ’111 kJ molвҒ»В№
- Equation (3): C(s) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g), О”H = вҲ’394 kJ molвҒ»В№

**Strategy:** The target equation has FeвӮӮOвӮғ as a reactant, but equation (1) has FeвӮӮOвӮғ as a product. So we must flip equation (1). The target has CO as a reactant, but equation (2) has CO as a product. So we must flip equation (2). The target has COвӮӮ as a product, and equation (3) has COвӮӮ as a product вҖ” we keep equation (3) as is. We also need the carbon atoms from equations (2) and (3) to cancel.

**Step 1 вҖ” Flip equation (1):**
FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s) вҶ’ 2Fe(s) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g), О”H = +824 kJ.

**Step 2 вҖ” Flip equation (2) and multiply by 3:**
We need 3CO as a reactant. Equation (2) makes 1CO as a product. Flip it: CO(g) вҶ’ C(s) + ВҪOвӮӮ(g), О”H = +111 kJ. Then multiply by 3: 3CO(g) вҶ’ 3C(s) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g), О”H = 3 Г— (+111) = +333 kJ.

**Step 3 вҖ” Keep equation (3) and multiply by 3:**
We need 3COвӮӮ as a product. Equation (3) makes 1COвӮӮ. Multiply by 3: 3C(s) + 3OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 3COвӮӮ(g), О”H = 3 Г— (вҲ’394) = вҲ’1182 kJ.

**Step 4 вҖ” Add all three equations:**

```
   FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s)       вҶ’ 2Fe(s) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g)         О”H = +824
   3CO(g)          вҶ’ 3C(s)  + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g)         О”H = +333
   3C(s) + 3OвӮӮ(g)  вҶ’ 3COвӮӮ(g)                   О”H = вҲ’1182
   в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
   FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s) + 3CO(g) + 3C(s) + 3OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 2Fe(s) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g) + 3C(s) + 3COвӮӮ(g)
```

Now cancel: 3C(s) appears on both sides, so it cancels. For the oxygen species: on the left we have 3OвӮӮ(g); on the right we have ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g) + ВівҒ„вӮӮOвӮӮ(g) = 3OвӮӮ(g). These also cancel completely. What remains is exactly: FeвӮӮOвӮғ(s) + 3CO(g) вҶ’ 2Fe(s) + 3COвӮӮ(g). This matches the target.

**Step 5 вҖ” Sum the О”H values:**
О”H = +824 + 333 + (вҲ’1182) = вҲ’25 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Why this makes sense:** The blast furnace reaction is known to be slightly exothermic. The value of вҲ’25 kJ molвҒ»В№ is small in magnitude, which is reasonable for a reaction where both reactants and products are relatively stable compounds.

### Worked Example 6: Formation of Hydrazine

**Problem:** Hydrazine, NвӮӮHвӮ„, is used as rocket fuel. Calculate О”H for the reaction: NвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮ„(l). This is actually the formation of hydrazine from its elements, so the answer is О”H_fвҰө of hydrazine. Use:
- Equation (1): NвӮӮHвӮ„(l) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ NвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l), О”H = вҲ’622 kJ molвҒ»В№
- Equation (2): HвӮӮ(g) + ВҪOвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ HвӮӮO(l), О”H = вҲ’286 kJ molвҒ»В№

**Strategy:** The target has NвӮӮHвӮ„ as a product, but equation (1) has NвӮӮHвӮ„ as a reactant. So we flip equation (1). The target has 2HвӮӮ as a reactant, and equation (2) has HвӮӮ as a reactant вҖ” we keep equation (2) but multiply by 2. We also need HвӮӮO(l) and OвӮӮ(g) to cancel.

**Step 1 вҖ” Flip equation (1):**
NвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l) вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮ„(l) + OвӮӮ(g), О”H = +622 kJ.

**Step 2 вҖ” Multiply equation (2) by 2:**
2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 2HвӮӮO(l), О”H = 2 Г— (вҲ’286) = вҲ’572 kJ.

**Step 3 вҖ” Add the equations:**

```
   NвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l)   вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮ„(l) + OвӮӮ(g)    О”H = +622
   2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g)     вҶ’ 2HвӮӮO(l)            О”H = вҲ’572
   в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
   NвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l) + 2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮ„(l) + OвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l)
```

Cancel 2HвӮӮO(l) and OвӮӮ(g) from both sides. What remains: NвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮ„(l). This matches the target.

**Step 4 вҖ” Sum О”H:**
О”H = +622 + (вҲ’572) = +50 kJ molвҒ»В№.

**Why this makes sense:** Hydrazine has a positive enthalpy of formation, meaning it is thermodynamically unstable relative to its elements. This is precisely why it is useful as a rocket fuel вҖ” it stores energy that can be released upon decomposition or combustion.

## 5. Enthalpy Cycle Diagrams: A Visual Tool

### What Is an Enthalpy Cycle?

An enthalpy cycle is a diagram that shows different possible routes from reactants to products. These diagrams are especially helpful for avoiding sign errors, because you can write down the two routes and set them equal to each other.

### The Formation Cycle

For a reaction using the formation method, the cycle looks like this:

```
             О”H (unknown)
Reactants в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Products
    в”Ӯ                          в”Ӯ
    в”Ӯ вҲ’ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants)      в”Ӯ +ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products)
    в”Ӯ                          в”Ӯ
    в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
              Elements
```

Going from Reactants to Elements requires reversing the formation of the reactants, so the enthalpy change is вҲ’ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants). Going from Elements to Products is the formation of the products, with enthalpy change +ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products). By Hess's Law, the direct route (Reactants вҶ’ Products) must equal the sum of the two-step route:

О”H = вҲ’ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants) + ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products)
О”H = ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants)

This is exactly the formula we used in Section 2.

### The Combustion Cycle

For the combustion method, the cycle is:

```
             О”H (unknown)
Reactants в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ Products
    в”Ӯ                          в”Ӯ
    в”Ӯ +ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants)      в”Ӯ +ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products)
    в”Ӯ                          в”Ӯ
    в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
         Combustion Products
         (COвӮӮ, HвӮӮO, etc.)
```

This time the two-step route goes down: Reactants burn to combustion products (+ОЈО”H_cвҰө reactants), then combustion products "un-burn" to form Products (вҲ’ОЈО”H_cвҰө products). Setting the routes equal:

О”H = ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants) вҲ’ ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products)

### Drawing Cycles in an Exam

A rough sketch of an enthalpy cycle can earn you marks even if your final numerical answer is wrong, because it shows the examiner that you understand the logic. Label each arrow with the chemical change it represents and the О”H value. Make sure the arrows form a complete loop. The cycle does not need to be beautiful вҖ” it needs to be logically correct.

## Answers

1. **Step 1 вҖ” Products:** ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) = 2(вҲ’394) + 3(вҲ’286) = вҲ’788 + (вҲ’858) = вҲ’1646 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Step 2 вҖ” Reactants:** ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants) = (вҲ’278) + 3(0) = вҲ’278 kJ molвҒ»В№. (OвӮӮ is an element, so its О”H_fвҰө = 0.)

   **Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:** О”H = вҲ’1646 вҲ’ (вҲ’278) = вҲ’1646 + 278 = вҲ’1368 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Why this makes sense:** This is the combustion of ethanol, and the value (вҲ’1368) matches the known О”H_cвҰө of ethanol. The large negative value confirms that alcohols release a great deal of energy when burned.

   **Common mistake:** Forgetting to multiply the О”H_fвҰө values by the stoichiometric coefficients. The coefficient of HвӮӮO is 3, so you must use 3 Г— (вҲ’286), not just вҲ’286.

2. **Step 1 вҖ” Products:** ОЈО”H_fвҰө(products) = 1 Г— (+9) = +9 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Step 2 вҖ” Reactants:** ОЈО”H_fвҰө(reactants) = 2 Г— (+33) = +66 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:** О”H = +9 вҲ’ (+66) = вҲ’57 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   The negative sign tells us that the dimerization (two NOвӮӮ molecules combining to form one NвӮӮOвӮ„ molecule) is exothermic. This means NвӮӮOвӮ„ is more thermodynamically stable than two separate NOвӮӮ molecules under standard conditions.

3. **Step 1 вҖ” Reactants:** ОЈО”H_cвҰө(reactants) = 2(вҲ’394) + 3(вҲ’286) + ВҪ(0) = вҲ’788 + (вҲ’858) + 0 = вҲ’1646 kJ molвҒ»В№. (OвӮӮ has О”H_cвҰө = 0 because it cannot be burned.)

   **Step 2 вҖ” Products:** ОЈО”H_cвҰө(products) = 1 Г— (вҲ’1367) = вҲ’1367 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Step 3 вҖ” Calculate О”H:** О”H = вҲ’1646 вҲ’ (вҲ’1367) = вҲ’1646 + 1367 = вҲ’279 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   This value should be the negative of the combustion enthalpy of ethanol (since this reaction is the reverse of combustion "from the elements' perspective"), and indeed |вҲ’279| вүҲ |вҲ’278|, the known О”H_fвҰө of ethanol. The small discrepancy is due to rounding in the given data.

   **Important check:** This problem asks for О”H, and the target reaction happens to be the formation of ethanol from its elements. So the answer should be О”H_fвҰө of ethanol. The formation method gave вҲ’278 kJ molвҒ»В№. The combustion method gives approximately the same value. This cross-check confirms that both methods are consistent.

4. **Strategy:** The target has 2NO as a reactant. Equation (a) has 2NO as a product, so flip equation (a). The target has 2NOвӮӮ as a product. Equation (b) has 2NOвӮӮ as a product, so keep equation (b) as is. NвӮӮ and OвӮӮ should cancel.

   **Step 1 вҖ” Flip equation (a):** 2NO(g) вҶ’ NвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g), О”H = вҲ’181 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Step 2 вҖ” Keep equation (b):** NвӮӮ(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 2NOвӮӮ(g), О”H = +67 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Step 3 вҖ” Add:**
   ```
   2NO(g)           вҶ’ NвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g)      О”H = вҲ’181
   NвӮӮ(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g)   вҶ’ 2NOвӮӮ(g)            О”H = +67
   в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
   2NO(g) + NвӮӮ(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ NвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) + 2NOвӮӮ(g)
   ```
   Cancel NвӮӮ(g). On the left we have 2OвӮӮ(g), on the right we have OвӮӮ(g). So one OвӮӮ(g) remains on the left. The resulting equation: 2NO(g) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 2NOвӮӮ(g). This matches the target.

   **Step 4 вҖ” Sum О”H:** О”H = вҲ’181 + 67 = вҲ’114 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **Why this makes sense:** The oxidation of NO to NOвӮӮ is known to be exothermic, which is consistent with the negative О”H.

5. **(a)** Target: C(s, graphite) + 2HвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ CHвӮ„(g). We need C and 2HвӮӮ on the left and CHвӮ„ on the right.

   Equation (1) has C on the left: keep it. C(s) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g), О”H = вҲ’394.
   Equation (2) has HвӮӮ on the left but only 1HвӮӮ: multiply by 2. 2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ 2HвӮӮO(l), О”H = 2(вҲ’286) = вҲ’572.
   Equation (3) has CHвӮ„ on the left, but we need CHвӮ„ on the right: flip it. COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l) вҶ’ CHвӮ„(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g), О”H = +891.

   Now add:
   ```
   C(s) + OвӮӮ(g)               вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g)           О”H = вҲ’394
   2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g)             вҶ’ 2HвӮӮO(l)          О”H = вҲ’572
   COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l)           вҶ’ CHвӮ„(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g)  О”H = +891
   в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
   C(s) + OвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) + COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l) + CHвӮ„(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g)
   ```
   Cancel COвӮӮ(g), 2HвӮӮO(l). On the left: OвӮӮ(g) + OвӮӮ(g) = 2OвӮӮ(g). On the right: 2OвӮӮ(g). Cancel. What remains: C(s) + 2HвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ CHвӮ„(g). This matches the target.

   О”H = вҲ’394 + (вҲ’572) + 891 = вҲ’75 kJ molвҒ»В№. So О”H_fвҰө[CHвӮ„(g)] = вҲ’75 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **(b)** The formation cycle for methane has three levels:

   Top level (left): Reactants = C(s) + 2HвӮӮ(g)
   Top level (right): Product = CHвӮ„(g)
   Middle level: Elements (the same as the reactants in this case, since the reactants are already elements)
   Bottom level: Combustion products = COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l)

   Route 1 (direct): C(s) + 2HвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ CHвӮ„(g), О”H = ? (the unknown).
   Route 2 (via combustion): C(s) + 2HвӮӮ(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g) вҶ’ COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l), then COвӮӮ(g) + 2HвӮӮO(l) вҶ’ CHвӮ„(g) + 2OвӮӮ(g).

   The cycle confirms: О”H = (вҲ’394 вҲ’ 572) вҲ’ (вҲ’891) = вҲ’75 kJ molвҒ»В№.

   **(c)** The student's claim is incorrect because a negative О”H_fвҰө does not indicate instability вҖ” in fact, it indicates the opposite. A negative О”H_fвҰө means that the formation of the compound from its elements releases energy, which means the compound is at a lower energy level than the separate elements. Lower energy corresponds to greater thermodynamic stability. Methane, with О”H_fвҰө = вҲ’75 kJ molвҒ»В№, is thermodynamically more stable than separated carbon (graphite) and hydrogen gas. If a compound had a positive О”H_fвҰө (like hydrazine at +50 kJ molвҒ»В№), that would indicate thermodynamic instability relative to its elements.
