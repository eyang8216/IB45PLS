# Unit 8: Redox &amp; Electrochemistry — Practice Problems

## Problem 1
Assign oxidation numbers to each atom in the following species:
(a) $\ce{MnO4-}$
(b) $\ce{Cr2O7^{2-}}$
(c) $\ce{S2O3^{2-}}$
(d) $\ce{Fe3O4}$

Show your reasoning for part (b), explaining how the oxidation number of chromium is determined.

## Problem 2
Determine the oxidation number of sulfur in each of the following species, arranging them in order of increasing oxidation number: $\ce{H2S}$, $\ce{S8}$, $\ce{SO2}$, $\ce{SO3^{2-}}$, $\ce{SO4^{2-}}$, $\ce{S2O3^{2-}}$.

## Problem 3
Consider the reaction:

$$\ce{2Fe^{3+}(aq) + 2I-(aq) -> 2Fe^{2+}(aq) + I2(aq)}$$

Identify the oxidising agent and the reducing agent. Explain the meaning of each term by referring to the changes in oxidation number that occur in this reaction.

## Problem 4
For each of the following reactions, determine whether it is a redox reaction. If it is, identify the species oxidised and the species reduced:
(a) $\ce{Zn(s) + CuSO4(aq) -> ZnSO4(aq) + Cu(s)}$
(b) $\ce{AgNO3(aq) + NaCl(aq) -> AgCl(s) + NaNO3(aq)}$
(c) $\ce{2H2O2(aq) -> 2H2O(l) + O2(g)}$

## Problem 5
The reaction of chlorine with hot, concentrated sodium hydroxide solution is an example of disproportionation:

$$\ce{3Cl2(g) + 6NaOH(aq) -> 5NaCl(aq) + NaClO3(aq) + 3H2O(l)}$$

Explain what is meant by the term "disproportionation," and by assigning oxidation numbers, show how chlorine undergoes disproportionation in this reaction.

## Problem 6
Balance the following half-equation for the reduction of dichromate(VI) ions in acidic solution:

$$\ce{Cr2O7^{2-} -> Cr^{3+}}$$

Show each step: balance chromium atoms, balance oxygen atoms by adding water, balance hydrogen atoms by adding $\ce{H+}$ ions, and balance charge by adding electrons.

## Problem 7
Balance the following half-equation for the oxidation of hydrogen peroxide in acidic solution:

$$\ce{H2O2 -> O2}$$

Use the systematic method: balance all atoms other than O and H, balance O with water, balance H with $\ce{H+}$, and balance charge with electrons.

## Problem 8
The manganate(VII) ion, $\ce{MnO4-}$, oxidises ethanedioate ions, $\ce{C2O4^{2-}}$, in acidic solution. The half-equations are:

$$\ce{MnO4- + 8H+ + 5e- -> Mn^{2+} + 4H2O}$$
$$\ce{C2O4^{2-} -> 2CO2 + 2e-}$$

Combine these half-equations to produce the fully balanced redox equation, and identify the oxidising agent.

## Problem 9
Balance the following redox reaction, which occurs in acidic solution, using the half-equation method:

$$\ce{Fe^{2+} + MnO4- -> Fe^{3+} + Mn^{2+}}$$

Show the two balanced half-equations and explain how they are combined.

## Problem 10
In a student's laboratory experiment, hydrogen peroxide, $\ce{H2O2}$, is added to an acidic solution of potassium iodide. The iodide ions are oxidised to iodine, $\ce{I2}$, while the hydrogen peroxide is reduced to water. Write the two half-equations, combine them to give the overall balanced redox equation, and identify the reducing agent.

## Problem 11
A voltaic cell is constructed using a zinc half-cell and a copper half-cell under standard conditions. The zinc electrode is immersed in $1.0\ \text{mol}\ \text{dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{ZnSO4}$ solution and the copper electrode in $1.0\ \text{mol}\ \text{dm}^{-3}$ $\ce{CuSO4}$ solution, with a salt bridge connecting the two half-cells. The standard electrode potentials are:

$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Zn^{2+}/Zn}) = -0.76\ \text{V}$$
$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Cu^{2+}/Cu}) = +0.34\ \text{V}$$

(a) Identify the anode and the cathode, justifying your choices.
(b) Write the half-equations occurring at each electrode and the overall cell reaction.
(c) State the direction of electron flow in the external circuit.
(d) Calculate $E^{\ominus}_{\text{cell}}$ and predict whether the reaction is spontaneous.

## Problem 12
A voltaic cell is set up with the following half-cells:

$$\ce{Ni^{2+}(aq) + 2e- <=> Ni(s)} \quad E^{\ominus} = -0.26\ \text{V}$$
$$\ce{Ag+(aq) + e- <=> Ag(s)} \quad E^{\ominus} = +0.80\ \text{V}$$

Calculate $E^{\ominus}_{\text{cell}}$, write the overall spontaneous cell reaction, and determine which electrode gains mass during operation of the cell.

## Problem 13
The standard hydrogen electrode (SHE) is assigned a potential of exactly $0.00\ \text{V}$. Describe the construction of the SHE and explain why it is used as the universal reference electrode. Given the following standard reduction potentials:

$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Mg^{2+}/Mg}) = -2.37\ \text{V}$$
$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Fe^{2+}/Fe}) = -0.44\ \text{V}$$
$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Cu^{2+}/Cu}) = +0.34\ \text{V}$$

arrange the metals $\ce{Mg}$, $\ce{Fe}$, and $\ce{Cu}$ in order of increasing strength as reducing agents, and explain your reasoning.

## Problem 14
A student constructs a cell using the following half-cells:

$$\ce{Sn^{2+}(aq) + 2e- <=> Sn(s)} \quad E^{\ominus} = -0.14\ \text{V}$$
$$\ce{Fe^{3+}(aq) + e- <=> Fe^{2+}(aq)} \quad E^{\ominus} = +0.77\ \text{V}$$

(a) Write the overall spontaneous cell reaction.
(b) Calculate $E^{\ominus}_{\text{cell}}$.
(c) Explain the function of the salt bridge in this cell, and state what would happen if the salt bridge were removed.

## Problem 15
Use the following standard reduction potentials to determine which species is the strongest oxidising agent and which is the strongest reducing agent:

$$\ce{Cl2(g) + 2e- <=> 2Cl-(aq)} \quad E^{\ominus} = +1.36\ \text{V}$$
$$\ce{Br2(l) + 2e- <=> 2Br-(aq)} \quad E^{\ominus} = +1.07\ \text{V}$$
$$\ce{Fe^{3+}(aq) + e- <=> Fe^{2+}(aq)} \quad E^{\ominus} = +0.77\ \text{V}$$
$$\ce{Zn^{2+}(aq) + 2e- <=> Zn(s)} \quad E^{\ominus} = -0.76\ \text{V}$$

Explain the general relationship between the sign and magnitude of $E^{\ominus}$ values and the oxidising or reducing strength of a species.

## Problem 16
Predict whether each of the following reactions will occur spontaneously under standard conditions. Use the standard electrode potentials provided:

$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Fe^{2+}/Fe}) = -0.44\ \text{V}$$
$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Fe^{3+}/Fe^{2+}}) = +0.77\ \text{V}$$
$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{Cu^{2+}/Cu}) = +0.34\ \text{V}$$
$$E^{\ominus}(\ce{I2/I-}) = +0.54\ \text{V}$$

(a) $\ce{Cu(s) + Fe^{2+}(aq) -> Cu^{2+}(aq) + Fe(s)}$
(b) $\ce{2Fe^{3+}(aq) + 2I-(aq) -> 2Fe^{2+}(aq) + I2(aq)}$

For each, calculate $E^{\ominus}_{\text{cell}}$ to justify your prediction.

## Problem 17

Draw a simple labelled diagram that highlights the key differences.

## Problem 18

## Problem 19

(b) Identify the species that are oxidised and reduced.
(c) Write the overall equation for the electrolysis and explain why the concentration of the sulfuric acid increases during electrolysis.

## Problem 20

Explain, in terms of the competing electrode reactions, why the products at each electrode are as you have predicted. The following standard electrode potentials are relevant:

$$\ce{Na+(aq) + e- <=> Na(s)} \quad E^{\ominus} = -2.71\ \text{V}$$
