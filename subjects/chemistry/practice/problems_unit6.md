# Unit 6: Chemical Equilibrium — Practice Problems (HL)

## Problem 1
Define the term *dynamic equilibrium* as it applies to a closed chemical system. State two macroscopic properties that remain constant when a system has reached equilibrium. Explain, at the molecular level, why these properties remain constant even though the forward and reverse reactions continue to occur.

## Problem 2
The reaction \ce{2SO2(g) + O2(g) <=> 2SO3(g)} reaches equilibrium in a sealed container at 700 K. The equilibrium mixture is found to contain 0.40 mol of \ce{SO2}, 0.20 mol of \ce{O2}, and 0.60 mol of \ce{SO3} in a volume of 2.0 dm³. Write the expression for the equilibrium constant Kc for this reaction, and calculate its value. State the units of Kc in this case.

## Problem 3
The equilibrium constant Kc for the reaction \ce{H2(g) + I2(g) <=> 2HI(g)} is 50.0 at 700 K. A reaction mixture at this temperature contains [\ce{H2}] = 0.40 mol dm⁻³, [\ce{I2}] = 0.10 mol dm⁻³, and [\ce{HI}] = 1.50 mol dm⁻³. Calculate the value of the reaction quotient Q. Determine whether the mixture is at equilibrium, and if not, predict the direction in which the reaction will proceed to reach equilibrium. Explain your reasoning.

## Problem 4
The esterification reaction \ce{CH3COOH(l) + C2H5OH(l) <=> CH3COOC2H5(l) + H2O(l)} has Kc = 4.0 at a certain temperature. Initially, 2.0 mol of ethanoic acid and 2.0 mol of ethanol are mixed in a sealed flask of volume V dm³, with no ester or water present. Construct an ICE table and calculate the equilibrium concentration of the ester, ethyl ethanoate. Express your answer in terms of the volume V.

## Problem 5
For the reaction \ce{N2(g) + 3H2(g) <=> 2NH3(g)}, the value of Kc at 500 K is 0.50 (units omitted). A chemist places 1.0 mol of \ce{N2} and 3.0 mol of \ce{H2} in a 2.0 dm³ container at 500 K and allows equilibrium to be established. Construct an ICE table and calculate the equilibrium concentration of \ce{NH3}. Assume that the change in [\ce{N2}] is x mol dm⁻³. (The resulting equation simplifies — use this to your advantage.)

## Problem 6
The equilibrium \ce{PCl5(g) <=> PCl3(g) + Cl2(g)} has Kc = 0.040 at 500 K. A 1.0 dm³ flask initially contains 0.50 mol of \ce{PCl5} and no \ce{PCl3} or \ce{Cl2}. Calculate the equilibrium concentrations of all three species. (You may assume that x, the amount of \ce{PCl5} that dissociates, is small enough that the approximation (0.50 − x) ≈ 0.50 is appropriate. Verify the approximation at the end.)

## Problem 7
Explain, with detailed reasoning, the effect of each of the following changes on the position of equilibrium for the exothermic reaction \ce{N2(g) + 3H2(g) <=> 2NH3(g)}: (a) increasing the total pressure by reducing the volume of the container, (b) raising the temperature, (c) adding more nitrogen gas to the equilibrium mixture, and (d) adding a finely divided iron catalyst. For each change, state clearly whether the equilibrium position shifts left, right, or remains unchanged, and explain why using Le Chatelier's principle.

## Problem 8
The reaction \ce{CO(g) + 2H2(g) <=> CH3OH(g)} has ΔH = −90 kJ mol⁻¹. Predict and explain the effect on both the equilibrium position and the value of Kc when: (a) the temperature is increased, and (b) a catalyst is added. In your answer, clearly distinguish between changes that affect the equilibrium position and those that affect the equilibrium constant.

## Problem 9
The reaction \ce{2NO2(g) <=> N2O4(g)} is exothermic. A sealed brown-coloured sample of \ce{NO2} gas is in equilibrium with colourless \ce{N2O4} at room temperature. Predict and explain the colour change observed when the sealed container is: (a) placed in ice water, and (b) placed in hot water. What does this tell you about the sign of ΔH for the forward reaction?

## Problem 10
For the endothermic reaction \ce{C(s) + CO2(g) <=> 2CO(g)}, state and explain the effect on the value of Kc of: (a) increasing the total pressure by adding an inert gas (argon) at constant volume, (b) adding more solid carbon to the equilibrium mixture, and (c) increasing the temperature. Explain why the addition of solid carbon does not affect the equilibrium position.

## Problem 11
The equilibrium constant Kc for \ce{2A(aq) + B(aq) <=> C(aq) + 2D(aq)} has a value of 1.0 × 10⁶ at 298 K. Explain the significance of this very large Kc value. What does it indicate about the composition of the equilibrium mixture and the relative rates of the forward and reverse reactions at equilibrium?

## Problem 12
A student investigates the equilibrium \ce{Fe^{3+}(aq) + SCN^{-}(aq) <=> [FeSCN]^{2+}(aq)}. At a particular temperature, the equilibrium concentrations are: [\ce{Fe^{3+}}] = 0.0030 mol dm⁻³, [\ce{SCN^{-}}] = 0.0040 mol dm⁻³, and [\ce{[FeSCN]^{2+}}] = 0.0060 mol dm⁻³. Calculate Kc at this temperature. The student then adds a few drops of concentrated \ce{Fe(NO3)3} solution. Predict the colour change observed and explain your prediction using Le Chatelier's principle.

## Problem 13
For the reaction \ce{H2(g) + Br2(g) <=> 2HBr(g)}, Kc = 1.0 × 10⁹ at 300 K. A reaction mixture has [\ce{H2}] = 0.020 mol dm⁻³, [\ce{Br2}] = 0.030 mol dm⁻³, and [\ce{HBr}] = 0.10 mol dm⁻³. Calculate Q and compare it with Kc. Predict the direction in which the reaction will proceed. Explain why even a reaction with a large Kc can proceed in the reverse direction under certain conditions.

## Problem 14
For the reaction \ce{2HI(g) <=> H2(g) + I2(g)}, the equilibrium constant Kc = 0.020 at 720 K. In a 1.0 dm³ container, 0.80 mol of \ce{HI} is placed and allowed to reach equilibrium. Construct an ICE table and calculate the equilibrium concentration of \ce{HI}. (Hint: the change term is 2x because of the stoichiometric coefficient of \ce{HI}.)

## Problem 15
The equilibrium \ce{CH3COOH(l) + C2H5OH(l) <=> CH3COOC2H5(l) + H2O(l)} is studied at two temperatures. At 298 K, Kc = 4.0. At 350 K, Kc = 2.0. Deduce whether the forward reaction is endothermic or exothermic. Explain your reasoning using Le Chatelier's principle and the relationship between temperature and Kc.

## Problem 16
The reaction \ce{2NO2(g) <=> N2O4(g)} has Kp = 6.7 atm⁻¹ at 298 K. Write the Kp expression for this reaction. A mixture at equilibrium contains \ce{N2O4} at a partial pressure of 0.80 atm. Calculate the partial pressure of \ce{NO2} at equilibrium.

## Problem 17
At 500 K, the equilibrium \ce{N2(g) + 3H2(g) <=> 2NH3(g)} has Kc = 0.50 (in appropriate units). Calculate the value of Kp at the same temperature. (R = 0.0821 dm³ atm K⁻¹ mol⁻¹.) Explain why Kp and Kc are not equal for this reaction.

## Problem 18
For the reaction \ce{H2(g) + I2(g) <=> 2HI(g)}, Kc = 50.0 at 700 K. Show that Kp = Kc for this reaction. Explain why this is the case, with reference to the change in the number of moles of gas, Δn.

## Problem 19
A mixture of gases in a 5.0 dm³ container is at equilibrium according to \ce{2SO2(g) + O2(g) <=> 2SO3(g)}. The total pressure is 3.0 atm and the mole fractions are: x(\ce{SO2}) = 0.20, x(\ce{O2}) = 0.10, and x(\ce{SO3}) = 0.70. Calculate the partial pressure of each gas and hence calculate the value of Kp. State the units of Kp.

## Problem 20
Consider the equilibrium \ce{A(g) + B(g) <=> C(g) + D(g)} which has ΔH = 0 (thermoneutral). A mixture of all four gases is at equilibrium in a sealed cylinder. State and explain the effect on the equilibrium position and on the value of Kc of: (a) compressing the mixture to half its original volume, and (b) increasing the temperature by 20 °C. Why does your answer differ from what would be observed for a reaction where the number of gas molecules changes?

## Problem 21
The reaction \ce{COCl2(g) <=> CO(g) + Cl2(g)} has Kp = 0.25 atm at 600 K. Pure phosgene (\ce{COCl2}) is introduced into an evacuated container at an initial pressure of 2.0 atm and the system is allowed to reach equilibrium. Construct an ICE table in terms of partial pressures and calculate the equilibrium partial pressure of \ce{CO}.

## Problem 22
Explain, with reference to the reaction mechanism at equilibrium, why a catalyst does not affect the position of equilibrium. In your answer, state whether a catalyst changes the value of Kc or Kp, and explain why the equilibrium composition remains unchanged even though the catalyst speeds up the reaction.

## Problem 23
For the equilibrium \ce{2A(g) <=> B(g) + C(g)}, Kc = 9.0 at a certain temperature. Initially, 1.0 mol of A is placed in a 1.0 dm³ container. Construct an ICE table and calculate the equilibrium concentrations of A, B, and C. (Hint: this problem can be solved by taking the square root of both sides of the Kc expression.)

## Problem 24
A student states: "Increasing the pressure of an equilibrium mixture of gases always shifts the equilibrium towards the side with fewer gas molecules." Evaluate this statement critically. Under what circumstances is it correct, and under what circumstances might it be misleading or incorrect? Give specific examples to support your answer.

## Problem 25
The reaction \ce{CaCO3(s) <=> CaO(s) + CO2(g)} reaches equilibrium in a sealed container. Write the Kc and Kp expressions for this equilibrium. Explain why the concentrations of \ce{CaCO3} and \ce{CaO} do not appear in the equilibrium constant expression. If the partial pressure of \ce{CO2} at equilibrium is 0.25 atm at 1100 K, calculate the value of Kp and state its units.
