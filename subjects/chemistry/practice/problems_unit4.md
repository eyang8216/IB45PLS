# Unit 4: Energetics & Thermochemistry — Practice Problems

## Problem 1
Classify each of the following processes as exothermic or endothermic: (a) combustion of methane, (b) melting of ice, (c) condensation of steam, (d) the reaction of barium hydroxide octahydrate with ammonium chloride at room temperature, (e) photosynthesis. In each case identify the direction of heat flow between the system and the surroundings.

## Problem 2
Define the following standard enthalpy changes and write the balanced thermochemical equation that each refers to: (a) standard enthalpy of formation of \ce{CO2(g)}, (b) standard enthalpy of combustion of \ce{C2H5OH(l)}. State the standard conditions for pressure and temperature.

## Problem 3
In a calorimetry experiment, 0.500 g of ethanol (\ce{C2H5OH}, M = 46.0 g mol⁻¹) is burned completely and the heat released raises the temperature of 200 g of water from 22.0 °C to 36.0 °C. The specific heat capacity of water is 4.18 J g⁻¹ K⁻¹. Calculate: (a) the heat absorbed by the water, in kJ; (b) the enthalpy of combustion of ethanol in kJ mol⁻¹. Explain why the experimental value is likely to be less exothermic than the literature value.

## Problem 4

Assume the density of the solution is 1.00 g cm⁻³ and the specific heat capacity is 4.18 J g⁻¹ K⁻¹. Calculate the enthalpy change for the neutralisation reaction \ce{H+ + OH- -> H2O} in kJ mol⁻¹.

## Problem 9

## Problem 10

## Problem 11

An iron catalyst reduces Eₐ to approximately 100 kJ mol⁻¹. Explain why the catalyst does **not** affect the equilibrium yield of ammonia, and why a compromise temperature of around 450 °C is used industrially despite the reaction being exothermic.
