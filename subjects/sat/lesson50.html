    • Day 7: Full practice test under exact conditions (same start time as real test)

    • Day 5: Drill 3 weakest topics (revisit those lessons)

    • Day 1 (Test Eve): Lay out clothes, ID, admission ticket, calculator, snacks. Sleep by 9:30 PM.

    Test Morning

    • Wake up at least 2 hours before test
    • Eat a protein-rich breakfast (not just carbs — you need sustained energy)
    • Arrive at test center 30 minutes early
    • Bring: photo ID, admission ticket, fully charged calculator (with Desmos if digital), water bottle, snack for break, extra batteries
    • Do NOT: drink excessive caffeine, study new material, check social media

    Part 3: Test-Taking Mindset

    The Difference Between 1500 and 1600

    A 1500 student knows the content. A 1600 student knows the content AND:

    • Catches every trap before falling for it
    • Never rushes but never runs out of time
    • Stays calm when a question looks unfamiliar
    • Treats every question as solvable (because they all are)
    • Double-checks answers using a different method

    Mental Reset Protocol

    If you hit a hard question and feel anxiety rising:

  • Breathe — 4 seconds in, 4 seconds hold, 4 seconds out
  • Flag it — physically mark it and move on
  • Answer 2 easy questions — rebuild confidence
  • Return — approach from a different angle (backsolve, Desmos, plug numbers)

    Part 4: Final Mixed Practice Test

    Instructions: 12 questions (6 math, 6 English). 15 minutes total. This simulates the cognitive switch between quantitative and verbal. Use all strategies from all 50 lessons.

    MATH (Questions 1-6)

    1. A car rental company charges $30 per day plus $0.25 per mile driven. If a customer's total bill for one day was $72.50, how many miles did the customer drive?

    2. The scatterplot below shows the relationship between hours studied and exam score for 12 students. The line of best fit is $y = 8.5x + 42$. What does the y-intercept (42) represent in this context?

    A) The average score increase per hour studied

    B) The predicted score for a student who studied 0 hours

    C) The number of hours needed to score 42

    D) The maximum possible score

    3. If $f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 7$, what is the minimum value of $f(x)$?

    4. A triangle has sides of length 7, 24, and 25. What is the measure of the smallest angle in degrees? (Round to the nearest degree.)

    5. The equation $x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 8y = 0$ represents a circle in the $xy$-plane. What is the radius of the circle?

    6. A researcher surveyed 500 randomly selected voters and found that 54% support a proposal, with a margin of error of ±4%. Based on this survey, which of the following is a valid conclusion?

    A) Exactly 54% of all voters support the proposal

    B) A majority of voters definitely support the proposal

    C) Between 50% and 58% of voters in the sample support the proposal

    D) It is plausible that between 50% and 58% of all voters support the proposal

    ENGLISH (Questions 7-12)

    7. The collection of short stories, which ___ published posthumously, won the National Book Award.

    A) was

    B) were

    C) are

    D) have been

    8. Unlike her predecessor, who favored aggressive expansion, ___.

    A) the new CEO's strategy emphasized consolidation

    B) the new CEO emphasized consolidation in her strategy

    C) consolidation was emphasized by the new CEO's strategy

    D) the strategy of the new CEO was consolidation

    9. The archaeologists discovered pottery fragments dating to the Bronze Age beneath the temple floor; ___, they found evidence of an even earlier settlement.

    A) however

    B) for instance

    C) moreover

    D) therefore

    10. While researching the effects of light pollution, a student takes these notes:

    • Study 1: Artificial light at night disrupts melatonin production in 87% of subjects
    • Study 2: Cities with LED streetlights reported 40% less light spill than sodium-lamp cities
    • Study 3: Nocturnal bird migration patterns shifted 15% away from brightly lit urban corridors
    • Study 4: Residents in high-light-pollution areas reported 23% lower sleep quality

    The student wants to emphasize the biological effects of light pollution on humans. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes?

    A) Light pollution affects both melatonin production and bird migration patterns.

    B) LED streetlights reduce light spill by 40%, and artificial light affects sleep.

    C) Studies have found that light pollution disrupts melatonin production in 87% of subjects and correlates with 23% lower sleep quality.

    D) Light pollution has been studied in relation to both urban planning and wildlife.

    11. In the passage, the author describes the old library as "a cathedral of silence, where even the dust motes seemed suspended in reverence." This description primarily serves to:

    A) criticize the library's lack of maintenance

    B) create a mood of sacred stillness and respect

    C) suggest that the library is too quiet to be useful

    D) emphasize the library's religious significance

    12. The data presented in the graph most directly supports which claim made in the passage?

    A) "The policy had an immediate and dramatic effect."

    B) "Gradual improvement was observed over the first three years."

    C) "The results were inconsistent and difficult to interpret."

    D) "No significant change occurred during the study period."

    *(Note: For question 12, assume a hypothetical paired passage + graph scenario. The correct answer depends on identifying which claim aligns with the data.)*

    Answers

    Math Answers

    1. Answer: 170 miles

    Explanation: $30 + 0.25m = 72.50$ → $0.25m = 42.50$ → $m = 170$. Quick check: $30 + 0.25(170) = 30 + 42.50 = 72.50$ ✓.

    2. Answer: B) The predicted score for a student who studied 0 hours

    Explanation: In $y = mx + b$, $b$ is the y-intercept — the predicted $y$ when $x = 0$. Here $x$ = hours studied, so $b = 42$ is the predicted score with 0 hours of study. (A) describes the slope (8.5). (C) reverses x and y. (D) is not what the intercept means.

    Wrong answers: A — slope, not intercept. C — reverses axes. D — not a valid interpretation.

    3. Answer: 3

    Explanation: Complete the square: $f(x) = (x^2 - 4x + 4) + 3 = (x-2)^2 + 3$. Since $(x-2)^2 \geq 0$, the minimum is when $(x-2)^2 = 0$, so $f(x)_{min} = 3$ at $x = 2$.

    4. Answer: 16°

    Explanation: Check: $7^2 + 24^2 = 49 + 576 = 625 = 25^2$ ✓ — it's a right triangle with hypotenuse 25. The smallest side is 7, so the smallest angle is opposite the 7. $\sin \theta = 7/25 = 0.28$. $\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.28) \approx 16.26° \approx 16°$.

    5. Answer: 5

    Explanation: Complete the square for both $x$ and $y$: $(x^2 - 6x) + (y^2 + 8y) = 0$. For $x$: $(x^2 - 6x + 9) = (x-3)^2$. For $y$: $(y^2 + 8y + 16) = (y+4)^2$. So $(x-3)^2 + (y+4)^2 = 9 + 16 = 25$. Radius $= \sqrt{25} = 5$.

    6. Answer: D) It is plausible that between 50% and 58% of all voters support the proposal

    Explanation: Margin of error means the true population proportion is likely within ±4% of the sample proportion (54%). So between 50% and 58%. (A) is wrong — "exactly" ignores sampling variability. (B) is wrong — "definitely" is too strong since 50% is in the interval. (C) is wrong — the interval applies to the population, not the sample.

    Wrong answers: A — ignores margin of error. B — "definitely" when 50% possibility exists. C — confuses sample and population.

    English Answers

    7. Answer: A) was

    Explanation: "Collection" is a singular noun (even though it contains multiple stories). The verb must be singular: "was." The intervening phrase "of short stories" and the clause "which..." are distractions. "Were" (B) is plural. "Are" (C) is present tense — the sentence describes a past event. "Have been" (D) is plural.

    Wrong answers: B — plural verb with singular subject. C — present tense with past context (posthumously = after death = past). D — plural.

    8. Answer: B) the new CEO emphasized consolidation in her strategy

    Explanation: "Unlike her predecessor" modifies the subject that follows. The subject must be "the new CEO" to parallel "predecessor." (A) compares predecessor to "strategy" — wrong. (C) compares predecessor to "consolidation" — wrong. (D) compares predecessor to "the strategy" — wrong.

    Wrong answers: A — compares person to strategy. C — compares person to concept. D — compares person to strategy.

    9. Answer: C) moreover

    Explanation: The first finding (Bronze Age pottery) and the second finding (even earlier settlement) are both discoveries that build on each other. "Moreover" adds information in the same direction. "However" (A) would show contrast — but both are discoveries. "For instance" (B) would make the second an example of the first — it isn't. "Therefore" (D) would make the second a consequence — it isn't caused by the first.

    Wrong answers: A — no contrast. B — second isn't an example of the first. D — no cause-effect relationship.

    10. Answer: C) Studies have found that light pollution disrupts melatonin production in 87% of subjects and correlates with 23% lower sleep quality.

    Explanation: Goal: "biological effects of light pollution on humans." (C) selects two human biological effects: melatonin (Study 1) and sleep quality (Study 4). (A) includes bird migration — not human. (B) includes LED streetlights — not a biological effect. (D) is too vague and doesn't emphasize biological effects on humans.

    Wrong answers: A — includes wildlife. B — includes technical, non-biological info. D — too vague.

    11. Answer: B) create a mood of sacred stillness and respect

    Explanation: "Cathedral" connotes sacredness and awe. "Suspended in reverence" directly suggests respect and stillness. The word "reverence" is the key — it means deep respect. (A) is wrong — there's no criticism of maintenance. (C) is wrong — the tone is positive, not suggesting uselessness. (D) is wrong — the library isn't literally a cathedral; the description is metaphorical.

    Wrong answers: A — no criticism implied. C — positive description, not negative. D — metaphorical, not literal religious function.

    12. Answer: B (for our hypothetical scenario)

    Explanation: Command of evidence (quantitative) questions require aligning the graph data with a specific claim. If the graph shows a gradual upward trend over 3 years, (B) is the correct match. (A) requires an immediate spike. (C) requires erratic data. (D) requires a flat line. The key skill: read the graph's trend, then find the claim that describes that exact trend.

    Wrong answers: A, C, D — each describes a trend that doesn't match the hypothetical data. The correct answer must precisely reflect what the graph shows.
