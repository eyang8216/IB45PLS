**1.** 🟣

Let $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$, where $a$, $b$, and $c$ are constants. If the graph of $y = f(x)$ has $x$-intercepts at $x = -2$ and $x = 5$, which of the following could be the value of $b$?

A) $-3a$  
B) $-2a$  
C) $3a$  
D) $7a$

**2.** 🔵

A grocery store sells apples at $\$2.50$ per pound and oranges at $\$3.00$ per pound. A customer buys a combination of apples and oranges totaling exactly 10 pounds and pays $\$27.00$. The customer wants to know how many pounds of apples were purchased, but the cashier made an error and rang up the apples at $\$3.00$ per pound and the oranges at $\$2.50$ per pound. Under this incorrect pricing, what would the total cost be for the same purchase?

A) $\$25.50$  
B) $\$27.00$  
C) $\$28.00$  
D) $\$30.00$

**4.** 🟢

A researcher collected data on the relationship between daily exercise time (in minutes) and resting heart rate (in beats per minute) for 15 participants. The mean exercise time was 42 minutes with a standard deviation of 12 minutes. The mean resting heart rate was 68 bpm with a standard deviation of 8 bpm. The correlation coefficient between the two variables was $r = -0.75$.

Which of the following statements must be true?

A) Exercising more causes a lower resting heart rate.  
B) At least one participant exercised for exactly 42 minutes.  
C) Participants with above-average exercise times tended to have below-average resting heart rates.  
D) The resting heart rate of each participant was within 8 bpm of 68 bpm.

D) $-6 \leq x \leq -1$

**18.** 🟢

|---|---|---|---|
| A | 100 | 5.2 | $\pm 1.1$ |
| B | 400 | 4.8 | $\pm 0.6$ |
| C | 900 | 4.5 | $\pm 0.3$ |

Based on the data, which study provides the most precise estimate of the true mean score increase?

A) Study A, because it has the largest reported mean increase.  
B) Study B, because it balances sample size and margin of error.  
C) Study C, because it has the smallest margin of error.  
D) All three studies are equally precise because they measure the same thing.
