SAT Context

Module 1 is the same for every test-taker. It contains a mix of easy, medium, and medium-hard questions. Your performance on Module 1 determines whether you receive the easier or harder Module 2 — and that decision controls your maximum possible score. If you get the easier Module 2, your math score is capped around 600 (out of 800). If you get the harder Module 2, you can score up to 800. The goal for Module 1 is not just "do well" — it's to qualify for the harder Module 2. This requires approximately 16–18+ correct out of 22. Every question matters. This practice module is designed to match the real SAT in difficulty, distribution, and question style.

Answers and Solutions

Question 1 — Answer: (C) 4

$5x - 3 = 2x + 9$ → $5x - 2x = 9 + 3$ → $3x = 12$ → $x = 4$.

  • (A) 2: $5(2)-3=7$, $2(2)+9=13$, not equal. Error: subtracted wrong.
  • (B) 3: $5(3)-3=12$, $2(3)+9=15$, not equal. Error: $12/3 = 4$, not 3.
  • (D) 6: $5(6)-3=27$, $2(6)+9=21$. Error: added 3 instead of subtracting? Or $3x = 18$?

Question 2 — Answer: (C) 10

$3(x-4) = 18$ → $x-4 = 6$ → $x = 10$.

  • (A) 2: $3(2-4) = 3(-2) = -6 \neq 18$
  • (B) 6: Thought $x = 18/3 = 6$, forgetting to add 4
  • (D) 22: $3(22-4) = 3(18) = 54 \neq 18$

Question 3 — Answer: (C) 3

Add the equations: $(2x+y) + (x-y) = 8+1$ → $3x = 9$ → $x = 3$.

  • (A) 1: Substituted $x=1$ into second: $1-y=1$ → $y=0$, but first: $2+0=2 \neq 8$
  • (B) 2: $x=2$ gives $y=1$ from second, but first: $4+1=5 \neq 8$
  • (D) 5: $x=5$ gives $y=4$ from second, but first: $10+4=14 \neq 8$

Question 4 — Answer: (A) $x > 4$

$3x - 7 > 5$ → $3x > 12$ → $x > 4$.

  • **(B) $x > -4$:** Sign error: $3x > -2$ → $x > -2/3$... various errors
  • **(C) $x < 4$: Inequality direction reversed
  • (D) $x < -4$:** Both sign error and direction error

Question 5 — Answer: (B) 6

Ratio: $\frac{3}{2} = \frac{9}{s}$ → $3s = 18$ → $s = 6$.

  • (A) 4: $9 - 3 - 2 = 4$ (incorrect operations)
  • (C) 8: $9/3 \times 2 = 6$... wait that's 6. Maybe $9 \times 2 / 3$ differently? Actually $9 \times 2/3 = 6$. Let me recalculate: $9 / 1.5 = 6$... Hmm, 8 would be $9 \times 2 - 10$? Various errors.
  • (D) 13.5: Used inverse proportion: $\frac{3}{9} = \frac{2}{s}$ → wrong setup

Question 6 — Answer: (B) 9

$f(7) = 2(7) - 5 = 14 - 5 = 9$.

  • (A) 2: Used $x = 2$ instead of $x = 7$
  • (C) 12: $2(7) - 2 = 12$ (wrong constant)
  • (D) 14: Forgot to subtract 5

Question 7 — Answer: (C) $x = 3$ and $x = 4$

$x^2 - 7x + 12 = (x-3)(x-4) = 0$ → $x = 3$ or $x = 4$.

  • (A) Both negative: Sign error — $(x+4)(x+3) = x^2 + 7x + 12$
  • (B) Mixed signs: $(x+4)(x-3) = x^2 + x - 12$, wrong
  • (D) Mixed signs: $(x+3)(x-4) = x^2 - x - 12$, wrong

Question 8 — Answer: (C) $4x^2 + 12x + 9$

$(2x+3)^2 = (2x)^2 + 2(2x)(3) + 3^2 = 4x^2 + 12x + 9$.

  • **(A) $4x^2 + 9$:** Forgot the middle term — $(a+b)^2 \neq a^2 + b^2$
  • **(B) $4x^2 + 6x + 9$:** Middle term: $2(2x)(3) = 12x$, not $6x$
  • **(D) $2x^2 + 12x + 9$:** Squared $x$ but not the coefficient 2

Question 9 — Answer: (B) \$40.50

25% off: \$60 × 0.75 = \$45. Then 10% off: \$45 × 0.90 = \$40.50.

  • **(A) \$39.00:** 35% off total ($60 × 0.65 = $39). But discounts are sequential, not additive.
  • **(C) \$42.00:** 25% off = \$45, then 10% of original = \$6 off → \$45 - \$6? No: used original for second discount
  • (D) \$45.00: Only applied the first discount

Question 10 — Answer: (B) 10

$g(2) = 2(2) + 1 = 5$. Then $f(5) = 5^2 - 3(5) = 25 - 15 = 10$.

  • (A) 0: $f(g(2))$ confused with $g(f(2))$: $f(2)=4-6=-2$, $g(-2)=-4+1=-3$. Not 0 anyway.
  • (C) 12: $f(g(2))$ computed as $f(2) \cdot g(2)$ or other error
  • (D) 28: $f(2) = -2$, $g(-2) = -3$, multiplied or added wrong

Question 11 — Answer: (A) 8

$3 + 2m = 19$ → $2m = 16$ → $m = 8$.

  • (B) 9: $19/2 \approx 9.5$, rounded down and forgot the flat fee
  • (C) 10: $(19-3)/2 = 8$... wait. $19/2 = 9.5$, not 10. Might have done $19 - 3 = 16$, $16/2 + 2 = 10$? Various errors
  • (D) 16: $19 - 3 = 16$, stopped there (forgot to divide by 2)

Question 12 — Answer: (A) $x^2 - 7x + 5$

$P(x) - Q(x) = (x^2 - 5x + 6) - (2x + 1) = x^2 - 5x + 6 - 2x - 1 = x^2 - 7x + 5$.

  • **(B) $x^2 - 3x + 7$:** Subtracted wrong: $-5x - 2x = -3x$? That should be $-7x$. And $6-1=5$, not 7.
  • **(C) $x^2 - 7x + 7$:** Sign error on constant: $6-1=5$, not $6+1=7$
  • **(D) $x^2 - 3x + 5$:** Both sign errors

Question 13 — Answer: (C) 80

$y = 5(4) + 60 = 20 + 60 = 80$.

  • (A) 65: $5+60 = 65$ (forgot to multiply hours by 5)
  • (B) 72: $5(4) + 52$? Various errors
  • (D) 84: $5(4) + 64$? Off by 4

Question 14 — Answer: (A) $\frac{3}{5}$

In right triangle $ABC$ with right angle at $C$, angles $A$ and $B$ are complementary: $A + B = 90°$. By cofunction identity: $\cos(B) = \sin(90° - B) = \sin(A) = \frac{3}{5}$.

  • **(B) $\frac{4}{5}$:** This would be $\cos(A)$ (using the 3-4-5 triangle)
  • **(C) $\frac{5}{3}$: Confused sine with cosecant (reciprocal)
  • (D) $\frac{5}{4}$:** Reciprocal of $\cos(A)$

Question 15 — Answer: (B) 5

$f(x) = (x-2)^2 + 5$. Since $(x-2)^2 \geq 0$, the minimum value is $0 + 5 = 5$, occurring at $x = 2$.

  • (A) 2: The $x$-coordinate of the vertex, not the $y$-value
  • (C) 7: $f(2) = 5$, not 7. Might have used $x=2$ and then $2+5=7$
  • (D) No minimum: Every quadratic with positive leading coefficient has a minimum

Question 16 — Answer: (A) 80

Let $s$ = student tickets, $a$ = adult tickets.

$s + a = 200$

$15s + 25a = 4200$

From first: $a = 200 - s$. Substitute: $15s + 25(200-s) = 4200$ → $15s + 5000 - 25s = 4200$ → $-10s = -800$ → $s = 80$.

  • (B) 100: Used $a = s$ (equal tickets), giving $15(100) + 25(100) = 4000 \neq 4200$
  • (C) 120: $15(120) + 25(80) = 1800 + 2000 = 3800 \neq 4200$
  • (D) 160: $15(160) + 25(40) = 2400 + 1000 = 3400 \neq 4200$

Question 17 — Answer: (B) $x + 3$

$\frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3} = \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x-3} = x + 3$ (for $x \neq 3$).

  • **(A) $x-3$:** Canceled $x$ terms incorrectly, or factored as $(x-3)^2$
  • **(C) $x^2 - 3$: Canceled only the 9? No coherent reasoning
  • (D) $\frac{x}{3}$:** Canceled $x$ and $9$? Completely wrong

Question 18 — Answer: (A) $500(3)^{t/4}$

Tripling every 4 hours: after $t$ hours, $t/4$ tripling periods. Expression: $500 \cdot 3^{t/4}$.

  • **(B) $500(3)^{4t}$:** Inverted the exponent — this would triple every $1/4$ hour (15 minutes)
  • **(C) $500(3t)^4$: Wrong structure — multiplies inside the exponent base
  • (D) $500(4)^{t/3}$:** Swapped 3 and 4

Question 19 — Answer: (B) 6

$(x+1)^2 + (y-3)^2 = 36$. $r^2 = 36$ → $r = \sqrt{36} = 6$.

  • (A) 3: Confused radius with center coordinate signs
  • (C) 18: $\sqrt{36} = 6$, not 18. Might have halved 36.
  • (D) 36: Forgot to take the square root — classic $r^2$ vs $r$ trap

Question 20 — Answer: (A) $(-1, 6)$

$f(x+3)$ shifts the graph left by 3: $(2, 7) \to (-1, 7)$. Then $-1$ shifts down: $(-1, 7) \to (-1, 6)$.

  • **(B) $(5, 6)$: Shifted right instead of left
  • (C) $(-1, 8)$: Correct horizontal, but shifted up instead of down
  • (D) $(5, 8)$:** Both transformations backwards

Question 21 — Answer: (C) 3

$\frac{4}{x-1} = 2$ → $4 = 2(x-1)$ → $4 = 2x - 2$ → $6 = 2x$ → $x = 3$.

Check: $x = 3$ doesn't make denominator zero. ✓

  • (A) 1: Makes denominator $1-1=0$, undefined. But also: $\frac{4}{0} \neq 2$
  • (B) 2: $\frac{4}{2-1} = \frac{4}{1} = 4 \neq 2$
  • (D) 5: $\frac{4}{5-1} = \frac{4}{4} = 1 \neq 2$

Question 22 — Answer: (B) 5

Set $x^2 - 4x + 5 = 2x - 3$ → $x^2 - 6x + 8 = 0$ → $(x-2)(x-4) = 0$ → $x = 2$ or $x = 4$.

Since $x > 0$, both qualify. Use the linear equation: $y = 2x - 3$.

For $x = 2$: $y = 2(2) - 3 = 1$.

For $x = 4$: $y = 2(4) - 3 = 5$.

Module 1 Strategy Summary

The Goal: Qualify for Harder Module 2

You need approximately 16–18+ correct out of 22 on Module 1. The exact threshold varies by test form, but missing more than 5–6 questions risks dropping you to the easier Module 2.

Pacing Checkpoints

| Checkpoint | Time Left | Questions Done |

|:---|:---|:---|

| After Q8 | ~29 min | 8 |

| After Q15 | ~18 min | 15 |

| After Q22 | 0 min | 22 |

Question-Type Quick Reference

  • Algebra (Q1–4, 6, 8, 11, 16, 21): Linear equations, systems, inequalities, equivalent expressions. These are your fastest points.
  • Advanced Math (Q7, 10, 12, 15, 17, 20, 22): Quadratics, functions, polynomials, transformations.
  • Problem Solving (Q5, 9, 13, 18): Ratios, percents, data, exponentials. Often word problems.
  • Geometry (Q14, 19): Trig and circles. Usually straightforward if you know the formulas.

If You Finish Early

  • Re-check questions 19–22 (hardest)
  • Verify unit consistency in word problems
  • Check for sign errors in your algebra
  • Make sure you didn't fall for $r^2$ vs. $r$ or $(1+r)$ vs. $r$ traps
