Lesson 38: Rhetorical Synthesis (Notes Questions)

What You'll Learn

The Rhetorical Synthesis question — also called "Notes" questions — is a brand new question type on the digital SAT. You'll be given a set of bullet-point notes and asked to choose which answer best uses information from those notes to achieve a specific rhetorical goal (like emphasizing a similarity, highlighting a difference, or stating a research finding). This isn't a grammar question; it's a thinking question. You'll learn a systematic strategy for matching the goal to the right combination of notes.

SAT Context

Rhetorical Synthesis questions appear in the **Expression of Ideas** domain on the digital SAT. They're unique to the digital format and typically appear a few times per test. These questions look unfamiliar to most students, but they follow predictable patterns. Master the strategy, and they become some of the easiest points on the test. Expect 3-5 Rhetorical Synthesis questions per test.

What a Rhetorical Synthesis Question Looks Like

You'll see something like this:

> While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

> - Note 1

> - Note 2

> - Note 3

>

> The student wants to [specific rhetorical goal]. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

Then you'll see four answer choices — each a sentence or two combining some of the notes.

The Core Strategy: A 4-Step Method

Step 1: Identify the Rhetorical Goal

Read the goal CAREFULLY. This is the most important step. The goal tells you what the answer must do. Common goals:

  • **Emphasize a similarity** between two things
  • **Emphasize a difference** between two things
  • **Introduce a study** and state its findings
  • **State a research finding** and its implications
  • **Compare and contrast** two approaches/phenomena
  • **Provide background** for a particular topic
  • **Define a term** and give an example
  • **Explain a cause-effect** relationship

Step 2: Identify Which Notes Serve the Goal

Not all notes are relevant. Your answer should use ONLY the notes that support the goal. Including unrelated notes is a common wrong-answer trap.

Step 3: Don't Add Information

The correct answer will NEVER include information not in the notes. Wrong answers often add extra details, interpretations, or claims that sound plausible but aren't supported.

Step 4: Check All Four Answers Against the Goal

Eliminate answers that:

  • Miss the rhetorical goal (even if all info is correct)
  • Include incorrect information not in the notes
  • Omit key information needed for the goal
  • Misrepresent the relationship between notes (e.g., stating a similarity when the goal asks for a difference)

> **🚨 SAT TRAP ALERT:** The most common trap: an answer that uses 100% correct information from the notes but doesn't accomplish the rhetorical goal. Always check: "Does this answer DO what the student wants to do?" A true statement isn't necessarily the right answer.

Common Goal Patterns

Pattern 1: Emphasize a Similarity

**Goal:** Emphasize a similarity between X and Y.

**What to look for:** Notes that describe X and Y in parallel ways. The answer should mention BOTH X and Y and highlight what they have in common.

**Wrong answers might:**

  • Only mention X or only mention Y
  • Emphasize a difference instead
  • Mention both but don't highlight the similarity

Pattern 2: Emphasize a Difference

**Goal:** Emphasize a difference between X and Y.

**What to look for:** Notes that show how X and Y diverge. The answer should present BOTH and use contrast language (however, whereas, unlike, in contrast).

**Wrong answers might:**

  • Present only similarities
  • Only describe one of the two
  • Include both but without contrast language

Pattern 3: Introduce a Study and Its Findings

**Goal:** Introduce a study and state its findings.

**What to look for:** Notes that describe who conducted the study, what was studied, and what was found.

**Wrong answers might:**

  • State findings without introducing the study
  • Include only the methodology but not the findings
  • Add interpretations not in the notes

Pattern 4: Support or Illustrate a Claim

**Goal:** Provide an example that illustrates a general claim.

**What to look for:** A general statement in the notes plus a specific example that matches.

**Wrong answers might:**

  • Give an example that doesn't match the claim
  • State the claim without the example
  • Misrepresent which note is the claim and which is the example

Detailed Examples

Example 1: Emphasize a Similarity

**Notes:**

  • The Amazon rainforest covers approximately 2.1 million square miles.
  • The Congo Basin rainforest covers approximately 1.6 million square miles.
  • Both rainforests are located near the equator.
  • Both rainforests are home to thousands of unique species.
  • Deforestation rates in the Amazon increased by 22% in 2022.

**Goal:** Emphasize a similarity between the Amazon and Congo Basin rainforests.

**Which notes are relevant?**

  • The location note ("Both rainforests are located near the equator") → similarity
  • The species note ("Both rainforests are home to thousands of unique species") → similarity
  • All other notes are about size differences or deforestation (not similarities)

**Correct answer style:** "The Amazon and Congo Basin rainforests share key characteristics: both are located near the equator and both are home to thousands of unique species."

Example 2: Emphasize a Difference

**Notes:**

  • Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy using carbon dioxide and water.
  • Cellular respiration converts chemical energy into ATP using oxygen and glucose.
  • Photosynthesis produces oxygen as a byproduct.
  • Cellular respiration produces carbon dioxide as a byproduct.
  • Both processes are essential for life on Earth.

**Goal:** Emphasize a difference between photosynthesis and cellular respiration.

**Which notes are relevant?**

  • The first two notes describe the processes differently (CO2 + water → energy vs. oxygen + glucose → ATP)
  • The byproduct notes (oxygen vs. CO2)
  • The "both" note is about similarity — NOT relevant for emphasizing a difference

**Correct answer style:** "Whereas photosynthesis uses carbon dioxide and water to produce energy and releases oxygen, cellular respiration uses oxygen and glucose to produce ATP and releases carbon dioxide."

Example 3: Introduce a Study and State Findings

**Notes:**

  • A 2021 study by Dr. Elena Rodriguez examined the effects of blue light on sleep quality.
  • The study involved 500 participants aged 18-35.
  • Participants who avoided blue light exposure two hours before bedtime fell asleep 45% faster on average.
  • Participants who used blue-light-blocking glasses reported deeper, more restful sleep.
  • The study was published in the Journal of Sleep Research.

**Goal:** Introduce the study and state its findings.

**Which notes are relevant?**

  • Who conducted it (Dr. Rodriguez, 2021)
  • What was studied (blue light effects on sleep)
  • The findings (fell asleep faster, deeper sleep)

**Additional details** (500 participants, journal, glasses specifics) may or may not be included depending on answer length.

**Correct answer style:** "Dr. Elena Rodriguez's 2021 study found that avoiding blue light exposure before bedtime helped participants fall asleep faster and experience more restful sleep."

Worked Example 1

**Question:**

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • The Industrial Revolution began in Great Britain in the late 18th century.
  • The revolution was characterized by the shift from hand production to machine manufacturing.
  • The Information Age began in the late 20th century.
  • The Information Age was characterized by the shift from analog to digital technologies.
  • Both transitions fundamentally changed how people work and communicate.

The student wants to emphasize a similarity between the Industrial Revolution and the Information Age. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) The Industrial Revolution, which began in Great Britain in the late 18th century, was characterized by the shift from hand production to machine manufacturing.

B) The Industrial Revolution and the Information Age both fundamentally changed how people work and communicate, although they occurred in different centuries.

C) The Information Age began in the late 20th century and was characterized by the shift from analog to digital technologies.

D) The Industrial Revolution began earlier than the Information Age, with the former starting in the late 18th century and the latter in the late 20th century.

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The goal is to emphasize a SIMILARITY. Option B directly states what they have in common: "both fundamentally changed how people work and communicate." Options A and C each describe only one of the two events — they don't compare or show similarity. Option D emphasizes a DIFFERENCE (timing), which is the opposite of the goal.

Worked Example 2

**Question:**

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • In 2019, marine biologists discovered a new species of octopus in the Pacific Ocean.
  • The species, named *Octopus vulgaris pacifica*, has eight arms and can change color.
  • Unlike most octopus species, it is active primarily during daylight hours.
  • Most octopus species are nocturnal.
  • The new species builds its dens using shells and small rocks.

The student wants to emphasize a difference between the newly discovered octopus and most other octopus species. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) In 2019, marine biologists discovered *Octopus vulgaris pacifica*, a new species with eight arms and the ability to change color.

B) *Octopus vulgaris pacifica*, discovered in 2019, has eight arms and can change color, and it builds dens using shells and small rocks.

C) Unlike most octopus species, which are nocturnal, the newly discovered *Octopus vulgaris pacifica* is active primarily during daylight hours.

D) Discovered in 2019, *Octopus vulgaris pacifica* is a new species of octopus found in the Pacific Ocean that builds dens with shells and rocks.

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The goal is to emphasize a DIFFERENCE. Option C directly contrasts the new species (diurnal) with most species (nocturnal) using "Unlike." Options A and B only describe the new species without comparison. Option D describes the species but provides no contrast with other species.

Worked Example 3

**Question:**

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • The Maya civilization developed a sophisticated calendar system.
  • The Maya calendar consisted of a 260-day ritual cycle and a 365-day solar cycle.
  • Maya astronomers tracked the movements of Venus with remarkable precision.
  • Their calculations of the Venus cycle were off by only two hours every 500 years.
  • The Maya civilization flourished in Mesoamerica from approximately 2000 BCE to 1500 CE.

The student wants to provide an example that illustrates the sophistication of Maya astronomy. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) The Maya civilization flourished in Mesoamerica from approximately 2000 BCE to 1500 CE and developed a calendar system.

B) The Maya calendar consisted of a 260-day ritual cycle and a 365-day solar cycle, demonstrating their understanding of celestial patterns.

C) Maya astronomical sophistication is evident in their tracking of Venus: their calculations of the planet's cycle were accurate to within two hours every 500 years.

D) The Maya tracked the movements of Venus, and their civilization also developed a calendar system that included both ritual and solar cycles.

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The goal is to illustrate the sophistication of Maya astronomy. Option C does this directly by citing the Venus tracking precision as evidence of sophistication. Option A doesn't mention astronomy at all. Option B mentions the calendar but doesn't highlight precision (just describes the cycles). Option D lists facts about both Venus and the calendar but doesn't frame them as evidence of sophistication.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • Black holes are regions of spacetime with gravity so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape.
  • Supermassive black holes exist at the centers of most galaxies.
  • The supermassive black hole at the center of the Milky Way is called Sagittarius A*.
  • Sagittarius A* has a mass equivalent to about 4 million suns.
  • Some supermassive black holes have masses equivalent to billions of suns.

The student wants to emphasize a difference between Sagittarius A* and other supermassive black holes. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) Sagittarius A* is the supermassive black hole at the center of the Milky Way galaxy.

B) Black holes, which are regions of spacetime with extremely strong gravity, exist at the centers of most galaxies.

C) The Milky Way's supermassive black hole, Sagittarius A*, has a mass of about 4 million suns, whereas some supermassive black holes reach masses of billions of suns.

D) Sagittarius A* has a mass equivalent to about 4 million suns and is located at the center of the Milky Way.

Problem 2

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • Jane Goodall began studying chimpanzees in Gombe, Tanzania, in 1960.
  • She discovered that chimpanzees use tools, which challenged the belief that only humans did so.
  • She observed chimpanzees using sticks to extract termites from mounds.
  • Goodall's mentor was the paleoanthropologist Louis Leakey.
  • Her research revolutionized the field of primatology.

The student wants to introduce Goodall's research and state its significance. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) Jane Goodall was mentored by Louis Leakey and began her research in Gombe, Tanzania.

B) In 1960, Jane Goodall began studying chimpanzees in Gombe, Tanzania, where her discovery that chimpanzees use tools revolutionized the field of primatology.

C) Jane Goodall observed chimpanzees using sticks to extract termites from mounds, and her mentor was Louis Leakey.

D) Chimpanzees use tools, which Jane Goodall discovered when she began her research in 1960.

Problem 3

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • The Silk Road was a network of trade routes connecting Asia and Europe.
  • It was established during the Han Dynasty of China, around 130 BCE.
  • Goods traded included silk, spices, precious metals, and glass.
  • Ideas and technologies, such as papermaking and gunpowder, also spread along the Silk Road.
  • The Silk Road remained active for over 1,500 years.

The student wants to emphasize that the Silk Road facilitated the exchange of both goods and ideas. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) The Silk Road, established around 130 BCE during the Han Dynasty, remained active for over 1,500 years.

B) The Silk Road was a network of trade routes connecting Asia and Europe that was established around 130 BCE.

C) Silk, spices, precious metals, and glass were among the goods traded along the Silk Road.

D) Along the Silk Road, merchants traded goods such as silk and spices, while ideas and technologies, including papermaking and gunpowder, also spread between Asia and Europe.

Problem 4

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system.
  • It is located off the coast of Queensland, Australia.
  • It stretches over 1,400 miles and covers approximately 133,000 square miles.
  • In 2022, a mass bleaching event affected over 90% of the reef's corals.
  • Coral bleaching occurs when water temperatures rise, causing corals to expel the algae living in their tissues.

The student wants to explain what happened to the Great Barrier Reef in 2022 and why. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) The Great Barrier Reef, the world's largest coral reef system, is located off the coast of Queensland, Australia.

B) Rising water temperatures cause coral bleaching by making corals expel the algae living in their tissues.

C) The Great Barrier Reef stretches over 1,400 miles and covers approximately 133,000 square miles.

D) In 2022, over 90% of the Great Barrier Reef's corals experienced bleaching, a phenomenon in which rising water temperatures cause corals to expel the algae in their tissues.

Problem 5

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • Marie Curie won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel).
  • She won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911 (sole recipient).
  • She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.
  • She remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.
  • Her research focused on radioactivity.

The student wants to emphasize Curie's unique achievement among Nobel Prize winners. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) Marie Curie won Nobel Prizes in both Physics (1903) and Chemistry (1911).

B) Marie Curie's research focused on radioactivity, for which she won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903.

C) Marie Curie remains the only person to have won Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields, receiving the award in Physics in 1903 and in Chemistry in 1911.

D) In 1903, Marie Curie became the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, sharing the Physics award with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel.

Problem 6

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • Miguel de Cervantes published *Don Quixote* in two parts, in 1605 and 1615.
  • *Don Quixote* is considered the first modern novel.
  • The novel follows the adventures of Alonso Quijano, who renames himself Don Quixote.
  • William Shakespeare wrote *The Tragedy of Othello* around 1603.
  • Cervantes and Shakespeare died on the same date: April 23, 1616.

The student wants to emphasize a coincidence involving Cervantes and Shakespeare. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) Cervantes published the first part of *Don Quixote* in 1605, and Shakespeare wrote *Othello* around 1603.

B) *Don Quixote*, widely considered the first modern novel, follows the adventures of Alonso Quijano, who calls himself Don Quixote.

C) Cervantes, author of *Don Quixote*, and Shakespeare, author of *Othello*, both died on April 23, 1616.

D) Shakespeare wrote *The Tragedy of Othello* around 1603, only two years before Cervantes published the first part of *Don Quixote*.

Problem 7

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

  • The Venus flytrap is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of North and South Carolina.
  • It captures prey using modified leaves that snap shut when trigger hairs are touched.
  • The plant takes approximately 10 days to digest a single insect.
  • After digestion, the trap reopens, ready to capture new prey.
  • The Venus flytrap is one of only a few plants capable of rapid movement.

The student wants to describe the Venus flytrap's process of capturing and digesting prey. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

A) The Venus flytrap, a carnivorous plant native to North and South Carolina, is one of only a few plants capable of rapid movement.

B) The Venus flytrap captures prey when an insect touches trigger hairs, causing its modified leaves to snap shut; the plant then takes approximately 10 days to digest the insect before reopening its trap.

C) The Venus flytrap is a carnivorous plant that uses modified leaves to snap shut.

D) Native to the wetlands of North and South Carolina, the Venus flytrap is a carnivorous plant that takes about 10 days to digest an insect.

Answers

Problem 1 — Answer: C

**Why C is correct:** The goal is to emphasize a DIFFERENCE between Sagittarius A* and other supermassive black holes. Option C uses "whereas" to contrast Sagittarius A*'s mass (4 million suns) with that of other supermassive black holes (billions of suns). This directly highlights the size difference.

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: Only describes Sagittarius A* without comparing it to others — doesn't emphasize a difference.
  • B: General statement about black holes — doesn't mention Sagittarius A* or any comparison.
  • D: Describes Sagittarius A* accurately but makes no comparison to other black holes.

Problem 2 — Answer: B

**Why B is correct:** The goal is to introduce the research AND state its significance. Option B does both: it introduces the research ("In 1960, Jane Goodall began studying chimpanzees in Gombe, Tanzania") and states its significance ("revolutionized the field of primatology") while including the key discovery (tool use).

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: Introduces the research but omits the significance and the key discovery.
  • C: Mentions the discovery and mentor but doesn't state the significance ("revolutionized primatology").
  • D: States the discovery but doesn't introduce the research or state its significance.

Problem 3 — Answer: D

**Why D is correct:** The goal is to emphasize that BOTH goods AND ideas were exchanged. Option D explicitly mentions both: "merchants traded goods such as silk and spices, while ideas and technologies, including papermaking and gunpowder, also spread." The "while...also" structure gives equal weight to both.

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: Only describes the Silk Road's establishment and duration — no mention of what was exchanged.
  • B: Only describes what the Silk Road was and when it began — no mention of exchange.
  • C: Only mentions goods — ignores the ideas and technologies half of the goal.

Problem 4 — Answer: D

**Why D is correct:** The goal is to explain what happened in 2022 AND why. Option D states what happened ("over 90% of the Great Barrier Reef's corals experienced bleaching") and explains why (the definition of coral bleaching: "rising water temperatures cause corals to expel the algae").

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: Only describes where the reef is — doesn't mention 2022 or explain coral bleaching.
  • B: Explains coral bleaching in general but doesn't mention the Great Barrier Reef or the 2022 event.
  • C: Only gives dimensions of the reef — no mention of 2022 or bleaching.

Problem 5 — Answer: C

**Why C is correct:** The goal is to emphasize Curie's UNIQUE achievement. Option C directly states the unique achievement ("remains the only person to have won Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields") and provides the details (Physics 1903, Chemistry 1911). "Only person" captures the uniqueness.

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: States she won both prizes but doesn't emphasize uniqueness — doesn't say she's the ONLY person to do so.
  • B: Only mentions the Physics prize — doesn't capture the two-field achievement.
  • D: Describes the "first woman" achievement, which is different from the "two scientific fields" achievement.

Problem 6 — Answer: C

**Why C is correct:** The goal is to emphasize a COINCIDENCE. Option C presents the coincidence: two great authors, Cervantes and Shakespeare, died on the same exact date. The "both died on April 23, 1616" directly highlights the remarkable coincidence.

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: Notes the proximity of their works' dates but not the death-date coincidence.
  • B: Only describes *Don Quixote* — no mention of Shakespeare or the coincidence.
  • D: Highlights a near-simultaneous timing of works (1603 and 1605) but misses the actual coincidence in the notes (same death date).

Problem 7 — Answer: B

**Why B is correct:** The goal is to describe the PROCESS of capturing and digesting prey. Option B provides the complete sequence: insect touches trigger hairs → leaves snap shut → 10 days to digest → trap reopens. This covers the full process from capture to readiness for next prey.

**Why others are wrong:**

  • A: Describes the plant's habitat and unique trait but not the capture/digestion process.
  • C: Only mentions the capture mechanism (leaves snap shut) but omits the digestion timeline and reopening.
  • D: Mentions habitat and digestion time but omits the capture mechanism and the trap reopening.
